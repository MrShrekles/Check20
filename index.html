How to Play
Introduction

Welcome to Check20, a tabletop RPG set in the roaring 1920s, blending high fantasy with the era's charm. Our game focuses on worldbuilding, fast-paced combat, and deep in-world relationships. It values player creativity and group consensus over rigid rules. Survival is key, as characters face real dangers, and not all battles are meant to be fought. Downtime is crucial, especially for roles like merchants needing time for their ventures. The game balances combat with social interactions, where NPCs offer rich stories and potential quests. It's a world where discovery, personal connections, and the thrill of combat coexist, against a backdrop of industrialization and magical mysteries. Players are storytellers, shaping the narrative with their actions in a world full of opportunities and challenges. Engage with the world, from running a jazz club to exploring treacherous lands, and let your imagination guide your journey.

Narrator

Your role is to create the game world, challenge players, and bring the story to life. Encourage player interaction and questions, and reward their achievements with points for character development. Flexibility and adaptation to player actions are key for a dynamic and enjoyable game. Foster creativity and collaboration for a shared adventure.

Players are Characters

Bring your character to life and collaborate with the group towards shared goals. Understand your character's abilities and be proactive in story progression. Think creatively and communicate effectively with the group for a rich, immersive experience.

How To Play

Engage in scenes set by the narrator, incorporating your character's background and goals. Interact with the environment and other characters, guided by the narrator's descriptions. If rules are uncertain, the narrator makes immediate, fair decisions, with adjustments made as needed. Focus on fun and collaborative storytelling for a rewarding experience.

Types of Scenes

There are several types of interactions that players may engage in during the game. These include relationships, mysteries, problems, exploring, combat, and downtime.

  • Relationships involve players interacting with characters from their own backstory or with new characters introduced by the narrator. These interactions can lead to intense role-playing and can bring new connections to the group. Creatures and NPCs may have basic or complex needs that players can exploit to gain something in return.
  • Mysteries and problems are presented by the narrator for the players to solve or interact with. These can range from missing children, bounties, or haunted houses that need to be dealt with. Exploring the world created by the narrator allows players to discover new areas and locations, and can lead to building relationships with local businesses or guilds.
  • Combat in this system is brutal, and players should be prepared for tough engagements. It is important to consider conditions and locking out creatures and NPCs from being able to act, rather than relying on hit points. Downtime involves characters recovering from battle and taking care of their equipment, which can lead to further interactions and connections within the city. Finally, character death is something to be expected in combat, but it does not necessarily mean the end of play. The group may consider seeking revival through a temple priest or hospital doctor.
  • Character Death is a potential outcome of combat, and players should be prepared for it. However, death is not necessarily the end of play. The group may consider seeking revival through a temple priest or hospital doctor, or may choose to continue the game with a new character.   • Downtime is an important aspect of the game, as it allows characters to recover from battle and tend to their equipment. It is important to not take downtime lightly and to consider it as a part of the overall story.

Overview

In the game, dice, particularly the d20, are used to resolve actions and outcomes. A roll of 15 or higher typically signifies success. Actions are categorized by duration, from immediate actions to whole-day tasks. When rolling, players add relevant stats known as Checks to determine outcomes, which range from failure to multiple successes (Success to Success +++) based on the roll. Higher successes may unlock greater effects or damage in combat. Failures present challenges but are not the end, offering narrative depth and problem-solving opportunities.

Opportunity Attacks

Opportunity Attacks occur when an attack misses or when a creature willingly moves out of your melee range, allowing the target a counterattack, which varies based on the initial roll's outcome. Advantage and Disadvantage rolls adjust difficulty, with double advantages or disadvantages resulting in automatic success or failure. Critical successes or failures bring memorable moments, potentially with significant effects as outlined on character sheets.

Opportunity attacks can also occur in ranged combat if the creature being attacked has a ranged option immediately available. Opportunity attacks will also trigger if a creature is within another creature's melee attack range and willingly leaves that range without taking the Disengage half-action.

Damage and Non-D20 Rolls

Damage calculation starts with armor, then wounds inward unless otherwise specified. Non-d20 rolls are used for damage, spells, conditions, and random events, with the narrator providing dice for these specific situations.

Advantage and Disadvantage

  • Advantage: Either roll 2d20 and take the higher number or +4. Take the higher result.
  • Disadvantage: Either roll 2d20 and take the lower number or +4. Take the lower result.
  • Locked Out: If you have double disadvantage on a roll, you are unable to perform the action.

Critical Hits and Failures

Critical hits can deal double damage, trigger additional effects, or even instantly defeat foes under specific conditions. Critical failures may result in reduced damage, equipment malfunctions, or other complications.

Criticals in Combat: Critical hits deal double damage, roll an additional time on the damage tables, roll on a random damage table, or instantly kill the creature if they are near death or if the situation warrants it.

Critical Failure: On a critical failure but still a hit, it should deal half damage, or the weapon could break, fall out of their hands, or create another complication on the battlefield. Perhaps the spell deals damage back or fails. A critical failure should not equal a success, but a creature with a +15 in a check should never really fail.

Opposed Checks

A niche roll is the opposed check, where two characters make rolls, adding their designated check against another creature or player making the same roll and adding their values as well. The winner of the check is the creature with the higher roll value.

Checks

In the game, checks dictate how your character interacts with the world and faces challenges. At the start, you're given 20 points to spread among these checks, but no check can receive more than 10 points from this initial pool.

The Math: When you roll a 20-sided die (d20), each outcome has a 5% probability. The average roll is 10.5, meaning there's a 50% likelihood of rolling 10 or above. Adding modifiers, like a +5 bonus, shifts the average roll to 15.5, enhancing your chances of superior results. As you move up the scale (15-34), outcomes improve, leading to better achievements in combat or surpassing original goals.

Your wounds are equal to your Agility + Strength checks. If you put points into Agility or Strength, it increases your wounds by that much. If you do not have any points in either, you have a minimum of 1. If you put 1 into one of the checks, it would still be 1 until you raise it above the minimum.

Loyalty

Loyalty not only measures individual trust and bonds but also reflects a character's reputation within factions or guilds, impacted by their actions and decisions. Positive deeds like completing quests or offering gifts enhance loyalty, while negative actions such as betrayal diminish it. This system includes:

  • Individual Loyalty: Loyalty between characters.
  • Group Reputation: A character's standing with factions or guilds changes based on their actions, affecting interactions and opportunities within those groups.

Loyalty points are earned or lost through these actions, dictating the character's relationship and standing within the community. Tiers range from Hostile to Unwavering to represent the depth of trust or disdain. Loyalty Checks, influenced by these points or the character's tier within the faction or guild, are pivotal in social encounters, leading to outcomes such as gaining access to unique resources, facing hostility, or unlocking special quests.

Combat

Combat simplifies to a clash between your party and enemies, initiating with an attack and proceeding in turn-based rounds, where strategy aims to incapacitate opponents akin to chess's checkmate, either through strategic disadvantage or elimination. Consideration for peaceful resolutions or stealth approaches can enrich the roleplaying experience.

Morale checks for enemies can dictate their willingness to fight, with living beings more likely to flee than undead. Turn order is determined by an initiative roll, which is a roll + Agility check, proceeding until one side wins or retreats. Actions can be delayed for strategic timing within one's turn, maintaining the chosen initiative position.

Theater of the mind combat descriptions can focus on relative distances: melee, reach, short, medium, long - rather than specifics, allowing for imaginative engagement with the scenario.

Character Health

Character health in this RPG pivots from traditional hit points to a system of armor and wounds for a grittier feel. Damage hits armor first, then wounds. Zero wounds result in being in the Injured Condition and further damage risks the Death condition.

  • Armor acts as your first defense, absorbing damage until depleted, then requires repairs. Some spells boost armor temporarily.
  • Wounds track minor injuries, healed by rest or medical aid. Exhausting wound capacity leads to the Injured condition; additional harm may cause death. Wound capacity is the sum of Agility and Strength checks.
  • Healing replenishes wounds up to the character's maximum, not affecting armor or directly curing conditions.

Press On

After a combat encounter, characters can "Press On" to regain 1 class resource, 1 spell point, repair armor for [[1d6]] + Crafting, heal for [[1d6]] + Survival, and remove all conditions and effects lasting for 1 minute or less. This option can be used twice per day, but not consecutively without engaging in another combat.

Long Rest

A long rest requires 10 hours of uninterrupted downtime, fully restoring all wounds, recharging class resources, repairing all armor to at least half, and removing any conditions that last one day or less. Characters can only take a long rest once per long rest and must be in a safe environment.

What You Can Do on Your Turn in Combat

  • Move: Utilize your speed and terrain to reposition, dodge, or engage. Movement can be split before and after your action as needed.
  • Action: The primary activity you perform, like attacking or casting a spell. Generally, you get one action per turn, though certain abilities may offer more.
  • Half-Actions: Smaller actions that can be doubled up in place of a full action, such as shooting and reloading a weapon.
  • Off-Action: Can be used to attack on your turn or react off your turn to a missed attack.
  • Free-Action: Minor tasks or communication that don't consume your action economy, like quick exchanges or item handling.

Combat and Non-Combat Mechanics

  • Finisher: This rule lets characters end a fight quickly if they attack unseen, if their foe can't fight back, or is caught off guard. A successful stealth check can confirm a silent, instant defeat, regardless of the enemy's remaining health. This option is ideal when facing a lone, defenseless target or an unsuspecting one in a vulnerable state, such as sleeping without guards.
  • Targeting: To attack, you must see, hear, or otherwise detect your target. Lack of senses may impose disadvantages.
  • Cover: Targets behind half cover are at disadvantage to hit; targets behind full cover cannot be targeted.
  • Surprise Turns: Attacking from stealth can grant an extra turn. Reveals the attacker's position unless abilities say otherwise.
  • Called Shots: Aim for specific effects rather than damage. Declare before attacking, accept a -10 to hit, and if successful, inflict conditions like Bleeding, Broken, or Blind, instead of normal damage.

Movement

In combat, movement is a separate type of action from attacking or using items. During any turn in combat, you can move any amount of your movement speed at any point during your turn. For example, if you have 30 ft of movement, you can move 10 ft to approach a creature, attack, and then move 20 ft away. However, if you engage a creature in combat and then retreat, they may have an opportunity to attack you using their Off-Action.

Fern-Tuft (ft) Definition: In this rulebook, "ft" does not stand for "feet" but for "fern-tuft," a unit of measure slightly smaller than the traditional foot. This ensures that when a character moves 50ft in a turn, the distance is more practical within the game's context. For ease, simply use "ferns" to refer to this unit of measurement in gameplay.

Types of Movement

  • Difficult Terrain: Requires twice as much movement to navigate, so every 5 ft becomes 10ft.
  • Jumping: Jump 5 ft from a standstill plus your Strength, or twice that distance with a running start.
  • Climbing: Make a Strength check to move up to half your movement speed in one turn. Failures result in falling; make an Agility check to save yourself.
  • Swimming: Swim at half your movement speed. Wearing heavy armor causes sinking.
  • Prone: Being prone gives melee attackers advantage and ranged attackers disadvantage. Standing up costs 15 ft of movement.
  • Moving Past Creatures: Move through a creature's space only if they are two sizes smaller (Tiny) or larger (Massive), or if they are allies who assist you.
  • Falling: Take 5 bludgeoning damage per 10 ft of falling distance. Falls that bring you to the Injured Condition cause the Death Condition instead.
  • Extreme Conditions: Hazardous environments, like lava or acid, cause 10 damage per round.
  • Burrowing: Creatures that burrow are immune to sight-based spells and non-piercing weapons. They need a way to breathe while burrowed.
  • Teleport: Special creatures can teleport to another unoccupied space without provoking opportunity attacks.

Actions

Actions are activities that take only a few seconds to complete. They are distinct from Half-Actions and Off-Actions, which have different characteristics and limitations.

  • Attack: Make a melee or ranged attack against a creature or object within range.
  • Unarmed Strike: Deals 1 BPorS damage and can be taken as an Off-Action.
  • Dash: Use your action to move again after moving, doubling your distance.
  • Grab or Hold: Make an opposed Strength check to impose the Pinned Condition.
  • Administer a Potion: Use an action to give a potion to a downed ally. Drinking a potion yourself is a Half-Action.
  • Stealth: Attempt to hide from creatures to gain an advantage on your next attack. Attacking reveals your position.
  • Cast a Spell: Requires sound, movement, and a spellcasting check (usually Spirit).

Half-Actions

Half-Actions consume half of your action economy and allow two actions in one turn.

  • Stances: Choose a stance to remain in until your next turn.
    • Advantage Stance: Gain advantage on your next attack.
    • Disadvantage Stance: Give disadvantage to enemies attacking you or allies in range.
    • Offensive Stance: Use a reaction for an opportunity attack if attacked.
    • Reaction Stance: React to failed attacks against you.
  • Disarm: Make an opposed Strength or Agility check to disarm a creature.
  • Help: Assist another creature, giving them advantage on a check.
  • Pick up an Item: Picking up an item is a Half-Action.
  • Use an Object: Interact with objects like levers or buttons.
  • Disengage: Move out of a creature's range without provoking opportunity attacks.

Off-Actions

Off-Actions don't require an Action or Half-Action and include quick reactions or minor activities.

  • Reaction: A special action taken out of turn, like an opportunity attack.
  • Opportunity Attacks: Attack creatures leaving your melee range without disengaging.
  • Drink Something: Use an Off-Action to drink or hand off a potion.
  • Disrupt: Impose disadvantage on an enemy's attack with a successful check.
  • Block: Reduce melee damage by half using a shield.
  • Demoralize: Force enemies to make a morale check with an Influence (Intimidate) roll.

Non-Actions

Non-Actions don't consume any part of your action economy but may be limited by the narrator.

  • Switch Weapons: Drop a weapon and draw another.
  • Stress Push: Take 4 stress damage to gain a +3 bonus on a low roll.
  • Roleplay: Briefly communicate or give orders without consequence.
Damage & Damage Types

In the world of Shek, numerous damage types exist, each with unique properties and effects. When a character rolls a success+ or greater with a weapon or spell, they have the opportunity to roll on the associated damage table, leading to a variety of outcomes. Here's an overview of the damage types and their potential interactions with armor, wounds, and injuries: These are organized by physical, elemental, and Realm Damage types. These are in each category not because of the amount of damage they do but what conditions and effects they cause on their damage tables. physical damage will often just deal additional damage while Realm Damage can cause immediate injuries or banish creatures to alternate planes momentarily. If an ability, weapon, gear, spell or whatever says it deals physical damage, it means a physical damage type of your choice. Same thing if it says elemental or realm. If you are given a choice at the time of creation of an item that usually means that item is locked into that damage type, unless you and the narrator determine that it is a shifting item or if it says so in the description. A weapon can only have one type of damage. If you have abilities that add damage to a weapon, spell or ability then it adds that on and doesn't change the type unless otherwise specified. A glyph can change the type of damage on a weapon. When you make a check with multiple successes then the weapon’s damage type is used, not the additional damage types.

Damage Types

Physical Damage

✊ Impact

The raw force of blunt weapons such as hammers, maces, and clubs delivers bone-crushing blows. This type of damage shatters defenses and can leave foes dazed and reeling, representing the sheer power behind a solid, heavy strike.

🎯 Piercing

Sharp and precise, piercing damage comes from weapons like arrows, spears, and daggers, which are designed to penetrate armor and flesh alike. Each thrust or shot aims for vital points, making it deadly in the hands of a skilled wielder.

⚔️ Slashing

Swords, axes, and claws inflict slashing damage, cutting through flesh and armor with swift, sweeping motions. This type of attack leaves open wounds and bleeding gashes, representing the ferocity and precision of edged weaponry.

Elemental Damage

🧪 Acid

Corrosive substances eat away at armor and flesh, leaving a sizzling aftermath. Acid damage represents the destructive power of alchemical concoctions, venomous creatures, and spells that dissolve whatever they touch.

🌑 Eclipse

Drawing power from shadows and dark magic, eclipse damage drains the essence of life and light. It encompasses the creeping death of necromancy, the suffocating embrace of smoke, and the chill of pure darkness.

🔥 Fire

Flames and explosions unleash intense heat, burning everything in their path. Fire damage is the fiery fury of magical fireballs, molten lava, and blazing infernos, leaving behind charred remains.

❄️ Ice

The biting cold of ice magic freezes the air and ground, numbing the flesh and slowing movement. Ice damage can encase enemies in frost, rendering them brittle and vulnerable to shattering.

⚡ Lightning

Electricity arcs and dances through the air, delivering shocking force. Lightning damage is the power of storms, crackling through the battlefield with speed and precision, often leaving scorch marks in its wake.

☀️ Solar

Brilliant light and divine radiance smite the unholy and cleanse the corrupt. Solar damage channels the power of the sun and holy magic, shining brightly to banish darkness and purify evil.

🌩️ Thunder

Thunder damage is the concussive force of loud, resonant sounds, capable of shaking the earth and shattering eardrums. It captures the essence of roaring booms, shockwaves, and the wrath of the skies.

☣️ Toxic

Poison and radiation inflict toxic damage, seeping into the body and corrupting it from within. It represents the slow, insidious nature of venomous creatures and noxious fumes, capable of weakening the strongest foes.

💧 Fluid

Water, blood, oil, and other liquids can overwhelm and suffocate. Fluid damage includes the crushing force of tidal waves, the searing burn of boiling liquids, and the asphyxiation of drowning or being engulfed by fluids.

Realm Damage

🌿 Nature

Harnessing the primal forces of the natural world, nature damage draws upon plants, animals, and the elements. It can manifest as thorny vines, swarming insects, or the relentless force of wind and rain, often bringing disease and decay to those who fall victim.

✨ Psychic

Psychic damage delves into the mind, bypassing physical defenses to inflict mental torment. It represents telepathic attacks, illusions, and the twisting of thoughts, leaving victims disoriented or shattered mentally.

☠️ Vozian

Born from decay, hate, and chaos, Vozian damage embodies the corrupting force of disorder and malevolence. It spreads disease and rot, unraveling order and stability, and often leaves a lingering taint on the soul.

❤️‍🩹 Healing

Unlike other damage types, healing repairs and restores, mending wounds and revitalizing the body. It draws upon positive energy and restorative magic, capable of bringing the gravely injured back to health, though it cannot cure conditions or ailments without special intervention.

Locks & Keys
  • Mundane Lock & Physical Key:

    A standard lock that requires a traditional Physical Key. It can range from basic padlocks to more complex tumbler mechanisms. These locks are susceptible to lockpicking or brute force.

  • Blood Lock & Blood Key:

    A Blood Lock requires the touch of blood to open. Depending on the difficulty, it might require the blood of a specific creature, a creature type, or just any generic blood. A Blood Key often takes the form of a dagger-like object that requires a cut to activate, channeling the essence of the wielder into the lock.

  • Soul Lock & Soul Key:

    A Soul Lock remains closed until touched by a specific soul linked to it. In more challenging versions, a soul must be sacrificed for the lock to open. The corresponding Soul Key may manifest as an enchanted crystal or token infused with the required soul or spiritual essence.

  • Trap Rune Lock & Rune Key:

    A Trap Rune Lock detonates when tampered with, layering protective runes that require an Arcane Rune Key to disarm. The key itself is often a magical glyph or talisman that matches the runes’ inscription.

  • Construct Mimic Lock & Password:

    The Construct Mimic Lock is a sentient mechanism resembling a lock but requiring a specific password to deactivate. The lock may interact with those attempting to open it, even bargaining or testing them for entry.

  • Illusionary Lock & Perception:

    An Illusionary Lock appears real but is not truly locked. Often, it hides a secondary trap, tricking the unwary into ignoring other dangers. Observant individuals or those skilled in perception can identify it for what it is.

  • Tech Lock & Code Key:

    A Tech Lock incorporates advanced mechanisms such as keypads, fingerprint readers, or voice-activated commands. These locks require a Code Key, which might be a passphrase, biometric data, or a specific tool like a data chip.

  • Basic Magic Lock & Spell Key:

    This lock relies on simple magical energy and requires a basic incantation or a low-power spell to unlock. The Spell Key might be a single word of power or a specific hand motion.

  • Celestial Lock & Star Key:

    A Celestial Lock glimmers with divine light or cosmic energy. It can only be opened with a Star Key, which might take the form of a constellation map, a blessed object, or a mote of starlight.

  • Chaos Lock & Entropy Key:

    A swirling, unpredictable Chaos Lock shifts and changes form, preventing easy access. The Entropy Key is equally erratic, requiring a random or illogical approach to use properly.

  • Chronomancy Lock & Temporal Key:

    A Chronomancy Lock is bound by time itself, only accessible at specific moments or when manipulated with a Temporal Key, such as a clockwork device or a relic attuned to time magic.

  • Crystal Lock & Resonance Key:

    Crafted from shimmering crystal, the Crystal Lock resonates with specific frequencies. A Resonance Key, often a crystal shard or tuning fork, must match the lock's frequency to open it.

  • Dragon Lock & Wyrm Key:

    Guarded by ancient draconic magic, the Dragon Lock often requires the breath or blood of a dragon to unlock. A Wyrm Key might be a scale, a claw, or a sigil carved in the language of dragons.

  • Elemental Lock & Element Key:

    The Elemental Lock binds its magic to one of the primal forces (fire, water, air, or earth). The Element Key could be a manifestation of the element, such as a flame, a vial of water, or a stone from a sacred place.

  • Nature Lock & Grove Key:

    A Nature Lock thrives with vines, wood, or blooming flowers. It requires a Grove Key, such as a sacred leaf, a druid's token, or a whispered prayer to the forest spirits.

Traps

Trap Power Levels (PL)

Trap PL is a modular system that allows narrators to scale traps for their desired level of danger or challenge. Each PL adds to the damage, range, or complexity of a trap. Use the following guidelines to adapt traps:

  • Damage: Traps deal 1d6 damage per PL (e.g., a PL 2 trap deals 2d6 damage).
  • Range: Trap effects scale by area or target range. A PL 1 trap has a range of 5 ft, increasing by +5 ft per PL. For example, a PL 10 trap would affect a 50 ft radius.
  • +Table: Every 3 PL adds a roll on the +Table for the damage type of the trap. For example, a PL 3 trap would act similarly to a Success+ on a weapon attack, adding effects like conditions or bonus damage.
  • Disarm: Traps can typically be disarmed with either a Physical check (e.g., Agility or Strength) or a Mental check (e.g., Intellect or Spirit), depending on the trap type.

Generic Trap Types

Below are common trap concepts for narrators to customize. Apply damage types, conditions, or thematic elements as needed.

  • Dart Trap: A hidden mechanism fires a projectile when triggered. Damage type can be chosen by the narrator (e.g., Toxic, Piercing, Fire).
  • Rune Trap: Magical symbols activate when disturbed, unleashing effects like explosions, curses, or elemental damage. Narrators can define the damage type (e.g., Fire, Lightning, or Psychic).
  • Trap Door: A concealed mechanism drops the target into a pit or hazard. Damage type depends on the environment (e.g., Impact, Spikes, or Lava).
  • Gas Trap: Releases a harmful cloud when triggered. Narrators can specify conditions (e.g., Coughing, Poisoned) and damage type (e.g., Toxic or Eclipse).
  • Net Trap: Launches a net to immobilize targets, applying the Pinned condition. Narrators can add enhancements like barbs (Piercing damage) or elemental effects.
  • Illusion Trap: Creates a deceptive or disorienting visual or auditory effect. Can impose conditions like Blind, Confused, or Frightened.
  • Explosive Trap: Triggers an area-of-effect blast. Damage type can range from Fire to Thunder or even Vozian for infernal-themed traps.

Trap Customization Ideas

  • Conditions: Add conditions like Coughing, Prone, or Blinded to enhance the trap’s complexity.
  • Triggers: Traps can be triggered by pressure plates, tripwires, magical detection, or proximity.
  • Themes: Adjust the theme of traps to fit your setting (e.g., jungle-themed vine traps, steampunk clockwork mechanisms, or infernal traps with cursed effects).
Merchant Conflicts

Merchant: Masters of Downtime

The Merchant class thrives not in battle, but in the spaces between. Downtime is their domain, where they build empires, craft unique goods, and influence the game world through cunning negotiation and resource management. Their impact on the narrative comes from creating opportunities, solving problems with commerce, and shaping the economy around them.

Merchant Conflict Hooks

As a merchant grows in power, their actions may disrupt local economies or provoke powerful factions. Here are potential challenges and hooks:

  • Criminal Syndicates: A faction sabotages operations with extortion or espionage, targeting trade routes or assets.
  • Merchant Guilds: Rival merchants or guilds lobby to impose regulations or dismantle monopolies.
  • Economic Collapse: Rapid expansion causes market instability, leading to price crashes or resource shortages.
  • Rebellion: Workers or customers revolt against unethical practices, requiring the merchant to address labor disputes or PR crises.
  • Political Intrigue: Nobles or governing bodies court the merchant for alliances, seeking influence over their wealth and network.

Merchant Downtime Activities

During downtime, merchants can engage in activities that enrich the narrative and offer unique gameplay opportunities:

  • Market Expansion: Establish new trade routes or expand operations into untapped regions. Success opens up new resources but may draw competition or attention from rival factions.
  • Crafting: Use skills like alchemy or blacksmithing to create unique items. High-quality or rare goods could attract buyers, leading to side quests or encounters.
  • Negotiation: Broker deals, forge alliances, or settle disputes. Successful negotiations might provide discounts, rare materials, or political favor.
  • Speculation: Invest in goods or businesses to manipulate the economy. Risk-reward scenarios can lead to significant profits—or devastating losses.
  • Infrastructure Projects: Build facilities like warehouses, workshops, or trade hubs. These structures provide tangible benefits, such as faster crafting or increased inventory capacity.
  • Merchant Intrigue: Hire spies or informants to gather intel on competitors, governments, or criminal organizations. This information could be leveraged for personal gain or to avoid conflict.

Merchant Reputation System

A merchant’s reputation shapes their interactions with the world, from loyal customers to envious rivals. Reputation can be tracked as a tiered system:

  • Tier 1 (Neutral): No special bonuses or penalties; standard market interactions.
  • Tier 2 (Respected): Merchants and buyers trust the player, offering minor discounts or favors.
  • Tier 3 (Trusted): Access to restricted markets, exclusive goods, or secret trade deals.
  • Tier 4 (Influential): Political figures or guilds seek alliances; rivals become increasingly hostile.
  • Tier 5 (Legendary): The merchant becomes a household name, influencing economies, nations, and even world events.

    Spellcasting

    Spellcasting

    In the intricate dance of spellcasting, every movement and utterance matters. A mage must channel their intentions through voice and gesture, like a maestro guiding an orchestra. Yet, magic demands clarity. If a caster finds themselves silenced or restrained, the symphony of magic halts. Even their gear plays a role; while hands must be free as the wind, a shield may hinder, and armor can stifle the very currents of magic they seek to harness.

    • Requirements: Spellcasting requires voice and gesture. Restraints or silence hinders magic.
    • Casting: Make a Spellcasting check. If you lack a specific Spellcasting check, use a Spirit check. Spells generally require an action to cast or double the spell points for half-actions or off-actions. Add +1 to no-cast spells.
    • Targeting: Requires sensory perception of the target.
    • Spell Points: Recover 1 when you press on and all on a long rest.
    • Prerequisites: Requires a minimum Spirit check of 2, granting 1 spell point.
    Basic Spellcasting

    Wordless magic is a basic form of spellcasting ideal for non-spellcasters or casting low-cost spells and illusions. It taps into an innate magical source without complex incantations, suitable for subtle, immediate effects.

    Defend

    Create a protective barrier around yourself or an ally, granting [[1d4]] armor (increases by [[1d6]] per spell point) until the end of your next turn. Barriers can be stationary or mobile with the caster and can have their duration extended by 1 minute per spell point.

    Devastate

    Make a spell check (usually Spirit) to damage a creature within short range, dealing [[1d4]] damage (increasing by [[1d6]] per spell point).

    Discern

    Detect hidden objects, creatures, or illusions within short range. For +3 points, focus on a creature to sense its motives, emotional state, or weaknesses. For +5 points, observe a distant familiar location, object, or creature for up to 1 minute. Reveal illusions for an additional +2 points.

    Enhance

    Boost a creature's next check by [[1d4]] (increase by [[1d6]] per spell point), or enhance a weapon's damage by +[[1d4]] until the end of their next turn.

    Light

    Create light reaching up to 50 ft per spell point. For +2 points, blind a creature within short range if it fails an Observation check. Alternatively, deal [[1d4]] Solar damage.

    Manifest

    Summon or create a creature, object, or barrier within short range for 1 minute. Barriers have 5 armor, increasing by 5 per spell point. Summoned creatures start at PL 0 and increase by +1 PL per 2 spell points.

    Simple Spell

    Perform a minor magical effect, such as warming or cleaning. Increase potency or range with spell points.

    Telekinesis

    Lift and move objects or creatures within short range, increasing size or speed per spell point spent.

    Advanced Spellcasting

    Advanced spells require significant spell points and are accessible only to skilled mages. These spells can alter reality, counter powerful magics, or manipulate realms, making them ideal for critical situations. They cannot be cast for free and often come with additional risks or conditions.

    Counterspell

    As a reaction to a spell or magical effect, spend 5 spell points and make a Spellcasting check at disadvantage (-4). Remove the disadvantage for an additional 3 points. On failure, the target spell’s effects are amplified.

    Decay

    Reduce the PL of a target within short range by -1 per 2 spell points until the end of their next turn. At 5+ spell points, impose conditions like Broken or Fear on suitable targets.

    Dispel

    Spend points to nullify magical effects: 1 for minor, 5 for moderate, and 10 for powerful spells. Pool spell points with other casters to dispel even stronger magic.

    Domineer

    Spend 1 spell point to read surface thoughts of a creature within medium range, 3 points to charm them for 1 minute, or 6 points to dominate and control their next turn. Add a telepathic link for +2 points.

    Imprison

    Spend 3 spell points to give a creature within short range the Pinned condition until the end of their next turn. Extend duration to 1 minute for +2 points, with escape attempts at disadvantage.

    Realm Shift

    Shift to an adjacent realm for 3 spell points. For 2 additional points per creature, bring others within reach range. Specify environmental or temporal changes based on the destination.

    Rejuvenate

    Touch to repair materials or heal for [[1d6]] per spell point. Can repair armor, cloth, and wounds equally effectively.

    Resurrection

    Bring back the dead by spending spell points: 4 for deaths within 1 minute, 8 for an hour, 16 for a day, and 24 for longer. On a failed check, the resurrected becomes undead.

    Structure

    Create a durable structure (e.g., dome or house) for 5 spell points. The structure has 100 armor and lasts for 10 hours. Expand its scale or defenses with additional points.

    Teleport

    Spend 2 spell points to transport yourself or a creature within short range. Extend the range or add multiple creatures for additional points. Line of sight is required.

    Translate

    For 1 spell point per creature, allow them to understand a common language for 1 hour. Translation is not perfect but enables basic communication.

    Transmute

    Transform a creature, object, or environment with a touch. Simple changes (e.g., water to ice) cost 1 point. Advanced or living transmutations require 3–10 points, with effects lasting until your next turn or permanently for 20 points.

    Spellcasting Notes

    Death Condition in Dynamic Magic

    To impose the death condition via spellcasting, a significant investment of spell points is required. Costs are determined by the combination of high-cost Manner and Transmission words. Upon casting, the afflicted target can attempt a survival check to resist the spell's effects on their turn. Success depends on the difficulty set by the Narrator, potentially influenced by the target's attributes or the spell's strength.

    The use of the death condition spell is carefully regulated by the Narrator to maintain balance and narrative tension, particularly during pivotal encounters. It should not be a trivial or dominant strategy but a deliberate choice with appropriate risks and costs. The death condition may also result from zero wounds, subsequent injury, and further damage, creating a layered approach to combat and spellcasting consequences.

    How Long a Magic Condition Lasts

    In Dynamic Magic, spell duration is influenced by the nature of the spell and the caster's intent. Temporary spells, like those causing prone or pinned conditions, last until the target counters them, such as standing up or making a strength check. These spells can become a battle of wills, with the caster needing to concentrate to maintain their effect.

    More severe spells, like those causing broken bones, have longer-lasting impacts that require medical or magical healing. Permanent spells, like those altering terrain or creating objects, remain without ongoing caster focus. Spell duration reflects the caster's purpose, the spell's character, and the balance within the magical world, with some spells easily countered and others leaving lasting effects.

    Armor

    When adventuring, armor is your primary line of defense. It's essential to ally with a blacksmith who can repair and upgrade your gear. In combat, armor absorbs hits before they affect your health. Remember, armor is not destroyed when its protection points are depleted; it simply can't offer protection until repaired.

    • Standard Repair: Utilizing a workbench, characters can repair armor, restoring half the total armor value + Crafting points per successful check per hour. This involves physical repairs like patching holes or replacing segments.
    • Magical Repair: Using spell points, repair [[1d6]] per spell point. This is an action requiring 1 spell point.

    Bulk & Items

    Bulk

    Bulk represents the weight and space your gear occupies, from negligible trinkets to burdensome hauls. It's not just about weight but also the size and how cumbersome an item is. Managing your bulk is crucial for maintaining agility and avoiding encumbrance.

    For example, a watch has 0 Bulk because it can be worn and forgotten about. Meanwhile, an empty barrel may be light but is awkward to carry, giving it a Bulk of 5.

    Bulk Classifications

    Bulk Description Examples
    0 Small/wearable items Watch, ring, eyeglasses
    1 Backpack-sized items Clothes, flask, small tools
    5 Large items Armor, barrel, large pot
    10 Heavy items Furniture, small canoe
    15 Very heavy items Piano, cart
    20 Extremely heavy items Small car, heavy safe

    Bulk Penalties

    • Bulk exceeds Strength: Over-encumbered (Pinned condition).
    • Bulk > 2x Strength: Over-encumbered + Exhausted condition.
    • Worn items: Worn items have no bulk.
    • Bundles: Bundles reduce the average bulk of items.
    • Backpacks: Reduce total bulk carried.

    Mounted Combat

    Mounted combat stands as a thrilling fusion of tradition and innovation.     Rules for Mounted Combat

    • Mount Movement: The base speed for beasts of burden such as horses is 60 ft. Speed for other creatures used as mounts will be determined based on their type and size.
    • Target Selection: Enemies may choose to direct their attacks at either you or your mount.
    • Dismounting & Mounting: Exiting your mount requires either the expenditure of 20 ft of movement or an action, and the same for mounting a creature or vehicle.
    • Mount Abilities: Different types of mounts provide various abilities and combat options. For example:
      • Warhorse: Might grant a charge ability that allows for a powerful attack after moving a certain distance.
      • Flying Mount: Could offer aerial movement options, but with limitations on how long they can stay aloft or how they maneuver.
      • Aquatic Mount: May allow for swift underwater movement and possibly an evasion bonus due to the fluid environment.

    Glyphs

    Glyphs

    Glyphs are powerful symbols infused with magic, capable of granting weapons, armor, and even characters with remarkable abilities and enhancements. Through glyphs, a simple piece of gear can become a legendary artifact, and a character may access abilities that significantly alter their role and capabilities within the game.

    Crafting and Application

    • Implementation: Crafting and applying glyphs require specific materials and a suitable crafting station, such as an enchanting table or magical lab. The rarity of materials and quality of the station can affect the outcome.
    • Crafting Process: Crafters must perform Crafting, Intellect, Spirit, or other related checks. The complexity of the glyph determines the number of successes required.
    • Application Process: Applying a glyph involves a similar check to integrate the glyph's magical essence fully. Each step takes one hour, with more complex glyphs requiring additional time.
    • Risk of Failure: Failed crafting or application checks can damage or destroy the item, with consequences scaling based on the severity of the failure.

    Glyph Tiers

    ✨ Basic Glyphs

    Enhance damage, armor, or checks, ideal for incremental improvements to gear.

    Requirements: 1 check to craft, 1 to apply; each step takes an hour and can be done with minimal equipment.

    • Glyph of Fortification: Increases armor by +2.
    • Glyph of Sharpness: Adds +1 to damage rolls on bladed weapons.

    ⚔️ Advanced Glyphs

    Introduce new mechanics like special attacks, defensive maneuvers, or unique effects.

    Requirements: 2 checks to craft and apply; requires a proper workstation and additional materials or assistance.

    • Glyph of Rebounding: Reflects half of damage back to the attacker as an off-action (reaction).
    • Glyph of Swiftness: Increases movement speed by +20ft when applied to footwear.
    • Glyph of Silence: Silences weapons or armor, ideal for stealthy characters.

    🌟 Legendary Glyphs

    Provide progression systems and grant game-changing abilities or major character transformations.

    Requirements: 3 checks to craft and apply; requires a powerful workstation and a team effort.

    • Glyph of Transformation: Allows the character to shift into a specific creature, gaining its abilities temporarily.
    • Glyph of Time Manipulation: once per long rest, take 2 actions on your turn.
    • Glyph of Elemental Mastery: Grants control over a specific element for strategic advantages.

    Glyphcrafting Failure

    When a failure occurs, the magical energies manifest as a complication. The crafter must resolve the issue by rolling on the Complication Table and taking specific actions to mitigate the effects.

    1d10 - Complication Table

    Roll Complication Resolution
    1 Magical Surge: Energy overflows, risking damage to nearby magical items. Perform a quick Intellect check to contain the surge.
    2 Elemental Anomaly: An elemental creature spawns from mishandled energies. Combat or negotiation may be required to dispel it.
    3 Arcane Feedback: Crafter suffers a backlash of minor health or mana loss. Perform a quick Spirit check to shield yourself.
    4 Material Corruption: Materials degrade, potentially ruining them. A Crafting check can salvage some or all materials.
    5 Unstable Rift: A small portal opens, drawing in nearby objects. Close it with a combined Intellect and Spirit effort.

    Curses

    Curses

    Curses add an exciting layer of strategy, tension, depth, and complexity to gameplay. By placing a curse on a character or item, players must weigh the risk versus reward of using it or benefiting from the curse's effects. While curses can grant powerful abilities or bonuses, they often come with significant drawbacks, creating a dynamic gameplay experience. Curses are not always malicious; they can stem from angels, tech lords, or other sources, adding an element of unpredictability and customization to the game.

    Curse Progression

    Curses work similarly to Paths and Talents. Players can spend XP to gain features from a curse, often at a steep cost. Some curses require players to acquire bane features before unlocking powerful boons. Curses offer a blend of complexity and customization, allowing characters to evolve uniquely.

    Word Burden: "Inscription of the Arcane"

    A Word Burden is an intricate curse bestowed upon those who dare delve too deeply into the mysteries of the spoken word. The body becomes a living parchment, etched with the glowing scripts of ancient and potent spell words. This curse serves as both a bane and a boon, challenging and empowering the bearer as they progress.

    Progression Steps

    1. Branded By Words: Your body is marked by glowing scripts of ancient languages, creating discomfort and distraction. However, these scripts faintly illuminate the area around you, providing a soft light in darkness.
    2. Lingering Echo: Every spell you cast leaves an echo in your voice for a short time, revealing your spellcasting to those who can interpret it. On the flip side, your understanding of spell echoes sharpens, granting advantage on Arcana checks related to identifying spells.
    3. Wordbound: Each spell inscribed on your body pulses with power when a corresponding spell is cast nearby, causing discomfort but alerting you to the use of that particular spell within a radius of 60 ft.
    4. Inscribed Resilience: As the power of the inscriptions intensifies, they start to strengthen your defenses, granting you additional armor equal to the number of spell words inscribed on you.
    5. Scripted Senses: Your senses become magically heightened, allowing you to see and hear things others can't. You gain advantage on Observation checks.
    6. Words of Power: The inscriptions start to resonate with your own magic. When you cast a spell that matches an inscription on your body, its potency increases, adding a bonus to the damage or healing amount.
    7. Woven Words: You can draw power from your inscriptions to cast spells without expending spell points. once per long rest, you can cast a spell that matches an inscription on your body for free.
    8. Inscribed Protection: The power of the inscriptions reaches its peak, creating a barrier around you. You gain immunity to one damage type of a spell inscribed on your body.
    9. Echoing Shield: Your inscriptions pulse with power whenever a spell targets you. As a reaction, gain immunity to damage from spells until the end of your next turn.
    10. Word Ascendancy: You and your inscriptions become one. The words covering your body shine brilliantly, and your eyes glow with arcane power. You gain immunity to all spell damage and the ability to cast a spell inscribed on your body as a reaction.

    6. Drain Vitality: As an action, make an unarmed strike against a living creature, dealing [[1d6]] vozian damage and healing for the same amount.
    7. Raise the Fallen: Raise the recently deceased as undead under your control for 1 hour. Control a number of undead equal to your progression step.
    8. Ghostly Form: Become incorporeal until the end of your next turn. Move through objects and creatures, taking half damage from physical attacks.
    9. Command the Dead: Control undead creatures within short range. Give a simple command as an action.

    Final Step - The Deathlord Ascension

    At the pinnacle of your curse, you become a Deathlord:

    • Regain 1 wound at the start of your turn.
    • Regain 1 injury every hour.
    • Control any undead you can see unless they are dominated by a stronger creature.
    • Gain immunity to Eclipse damage.
    • If you take the death condition, you fall unconscious for [[1d6]] weeks. If your head remains intact, you reform with full wounds and injuries, reassembling yourself if needed.
    Dark Curse

    Dark Curse

    This curse, often linked to the Forsaken Mystic class path, brings random, escalating afflictions. Each step forces a roll on the table below, leading to unpredictable and often harrowing consequences.

    Progression Steps

    Roll on the table for each step. If a result is already taken, adjust as follows:

    • Odd: Choose 1 progression step above the rolled result.
    • Even: Choose 1 progression step below the rolled result.
    • If neither applies: On odd rolls, gain -1 to all stats (unless already at 0). On even rolls, gain +1 to all stats.

    Progression Table

    1. Eternal Night: You can only see in dim light or darkness. Bright light blinds you, causing disadvantage on Observation checks in daylight.
    2. Shadow's Embrace: Your shadow attacks allies during combat on a roll of 1 on a d6 when rolling initiative.
    3. Decaying Touch: Anything you touch withers or dies. Healing spells and potions cannot be applied by you.
    4. Haunted: You see terrifying apparitions, causing disadvantage on Observation checks and requiring a Spirit check each night to benefit from rest.
    5. Unending Thirst: You must consume twice as much water daily and suffer exhaustion faster in extreme heat.
    6. Pain Amplification: Take 1 additional point of damage from all sources.
    7. Eternal Wound: A bleeding wound deals [[1d4]] necrotic damage at the start of your turn.
    8. Voice of Discord: Gain disadvantage on Influence checks but advantage on Intimidate checks.
    9. Marked by Death: Spectral reapers attack you each long rest, with a challenge rating of 1/4 of your level.
    10. Uncontrollable Rage: On a d20 roll of 1-2 after a long rest, enter a barbarian-like rage for the day.
    11. Living Nightmare: Suffer terrifying illusions, requiring a Spirit check each combat or become frightened.
    12. Cursed Transformation: On full moons, transform into a beast and lose control for the night.
    13. Spectral Chains: Your speed is reduced by 10 ft, and you cannot Dash.
    14. Life Drain: Age 1 year per day, dying upon reaching your race's maximum age.
    15. Doomed Love: Romantic partners are destined for tragedy at the GM's discretion.
    16. Eternal Silence: You cannot make sound, failing all checks requiring speech or noise.
    17. Memory Decay: Lose personal memories, suffering disadvantage on related Intellect checks.
    18. Unending Despair: Always considered under the "dispirited" condition, with disadvantage on Spirit checks.
    19. Invisible Fiend: An unseen entity attacks you once per long rest for [[1d8]] psychic damage.
    20. Dark Prophecy: Visions of disaster haunt you, requiring Observation checks to discern reality and dealing 1 psychic damage on failure.
    Technomancer's Oath

    Technomancer's Oath

    The allure of combining arcane magic and technological prowess has enticed you down a dangerous path. Bit by bit, your body is replaced with machinery, your consciousness intertwined with arcane energies and electronic impulses. With each step, the boundary between the physical and digital, the magical and mechanical, blurs.

    Curse Progression

    1. Cybernetic Symbiosis: Your limbs begin to be replaced by arcane-imbued technology. Gain advantage on Strength or Agility checks relying on the replaced limbs. However, take double damage from Lightning damage.
    2. Arcane Processor: A portion of your brain is replaced by a magic-tech processor. Gain advantage on Intellect and Observation checks, but exposure to powerful magic or Lightning damage gives you the confused condition for 1 minute.
    3. Optical Enhancement: Your eyes are replaced with advanced optical sensors. See in dim light as if it were bright light and in complete darkness as if it were dim light. You can zoom to see far distances. However, flash bangs and sudden bright magical lights can blind you for 1 minute.
    4. Aetheric Heart: Your heart is replaced with a magical-tech reactor, removing the need for food and sleep. Gain resistance to poison and disease, but you're more susceptible to magic drain or anti-magic fields, which cause a random condition (2) from the table below for 1 minute.
    5. Mechano-Magical Network: Your nervous system is upgraded, heightening reflexes and magical interaction. Remove the negative effects from the previous progression steps.

      6. Greater Progression

      As you dive deeper into the mysteries of technomancy, the lines blur further. You are neither fully organic nor fully mechanical, but something new - an embodiment of magic and machine intertwined. Each upgrade strengthens your control over technology and magic, granting you unprecedented abilities. However, these steps also mark the increasing disconnect between your current self and your past humanity, with vulnerabilities rooted in your dual nature. Harnessing the full potential of this path may lead to unprecedented power or a profound existential crisis, and it's up to you to navigate this treacherous journey.

    6. Integrated Arcane Arsenal: Integrate magical weapons into your mechanized body parts. These weapons can be summoned and dismissed at will.
    7. Spectral Firewall: As a half-action, create a protective arcane shield around you that prevents the next source of damage until the end of your next turn.
    8. Digital Consciousness: Upload your consciousness into digital devices, cybernetics, constructs, or anything else with a tech-magic center. If you are more powerful, you can overtake or upgrade the device and take control.
    9. Mananite Restoration: Mananites circulate through your body, rapidly repairing physical damage. Heal 1 hit point every minute and 1 injury every hour.
    10. Singularity Ascension: Your transformation reaches its zenith as you become an entity of pure techno-magical energy. Gain the ability to control and shape arcane-tech devices in your vicinity, but the stability of your new form heavily relies on the availability of arcane and technological resources.

    Infernal Skritches

    Infernal Skritches

    The touch of the damned leaves more than a physical scar; it marks the soul. Infernal Skritches is a potent affliction transmitted through the rending claws and gnashing teeth of true demons. This curse burns away mortal flesh, replacing it with the accursed essence of the abyss.

    Infection

    After an attack from a true demon (bite, claw, or scratch on exposed flesh), the character must make a Survival check. On failure, they've contracted Infernal Skritches. The effects aren't immediate and manifest after [[1d4]] days.

    Disease Progression

    1. Infernal Mark: The wound becomes hot to the touch and scabbed over, yet the victim feels no pain. Gain the ability to understand and speak Infernal and cast simple Infernal spells, but suffer a -2 penalty on Influence checks with celestial beings.
    2. Festering Wound: The wound festers and burns. Each time the afflicted takes damage, they gain another Infernal wound. Infernal magic becomes more potent, but they suffer disadvantage on Influence and Observation checks due to their partially demonic presence.
    3. Demonic Transformation: Physical traits of a demon begin to manifest, such as horns, claws, or a tail. You can speak any Vozian language, but Infernal wounds cannot be healed by non-infernal means.

      4. Once all 3 steps are taken: Gain Demonic Resilience: Immunity to disease, regeneration of 1 wound every 3 hours, and healing spells harm you instead of healing you.

      Greater Progression

      As the demonic essence takes hold, you can remove previous disadvantages by progressing further. Unlock these features in any order:

    4. Infernal Ascendance: Cast any infernal spell at max level without using additional spell slots once per Press On.
    5. Demonic Form: Transform into a true demonic form. In this form, gain advantage on Strength and Agility checks and a flight speed equal to your movement speed.
    6. Infernal Pact: Form contracts binding others to your will. As an action, offer a pact to a willing creature, obligating them to perform a task for a favor or boon of your choice.
    7. Abyssal Command: Command lesser demons within short range. As an action, give a command that demons will follow to the best of their ability.
    8. Infernal Translocation: Teleport through Vozo's hellish dimension to a location you can see within medium range as an action.
    9. Vozo's Whispers: Whisper dark thoughts into a creature's mind, causing it to doubt or act against its interests. A creature within short range must make an Intellect check against yours or follow a simple command on its next turn.

    Final Step - Demonic Overlord

    At the pinnacle of the disease, you become a full-fledged Demonic Overlord:

    • Summon lesser demons at will.
    • Infernal spells are cast at max level without using additional spell slots.
    • Immunity to all non-celestial magics.
    • If killed, your essence is banished to the infernal realm, where you are reborn in the infernal pits.
    Sanguine Fever

    Sanguine Fever

    Blood isn't just sustenance for vampires; it's a transformative essence. The Vampiric Sanguine Fever is transmitted through the envenomed fangs of true vampires or consuming strigoi blood, and it alters the body and soul, infusing it with the eternal night of vampirism.

    Infection

    Following a vampire's bite or by drinking strigoi blood, the victim may contract the disease. Symptoms take time to manifest as the curse reshapes the afflicted.

    Disease Progression

    1. Sunlight Sensitivity: Skin becomes pale and sensitive. You suffer a -2 penalty on physical action checks in direct sunlight but gain darkvision, seeing perfectly in the dark.
    2. Blood Thirst: You crave fresh blood. Drinking blood grants a temporary boost to physical stats, but abstaining imposes penalties to those stats.
    3. Vampiric Transformation: Canines elongate into fangs, and traits like red eyes or cold skin appear. Gain the ability to charm with a gaze, but you can only consume blood for sustenance.

      4. Once all 3 steps are taken: Gain Vampiric Resilience: Immunity to non-magical diseases, regeneration of 1 wound every 6 hours, but healing spells no longer affect you.

      Greater Progression

      As the vampiric essence intensifies, remove previous disadvantages and unlock the following features in any order:

    4. Mist Form: Transform into an ethereal mist, gaining the intangible condition.
    5. Bat Transformation: Transform into a bat, gaining the ability to fly.
    6. Vampiric Charm: Strengthened charm ability, making it harder for victims to resist your hypnotic gaze.
    7. Night's Embrace: Drastically increased strength, speed, and agility in darkness or nighttime.
    8. Blood Bond: By feeding someone your blood, create a mental link with them to sense emotions and issue telepathic commands.
    9. Coffin Sanctuary: Designate a coffin as your sanctuary. While inside, you are nearly invulnerable and heal rapidly.

    Final Step - Vampire Lord/Lady

    At the zenith of your transformation, you become a Vampire Lord or Lady:

    • Command lesser vampires and nocturnal creatures.
    • Your charm becomes nearly irresistible.
    • Your physical prowess is unmatched in the night.
    • Sunlight becomes your dire enemy, causing grievous harm on exposure.
    • If killed, your spirit binds to your Coffin Sanctuary, allowing resurrection with enough time and blood.
    Verdant Fever

    Verdant Fever

    Verdant Fever is contracted through prolonged exposure to magical forests or nature damage. The disease begins to transform the victim into a plant-like creature, entwining them with the natural world.

    Disease Progression

    1. Green Onset: Skin turns green and rough, impairing motor skills. You suffer -2 to Agility.
    2. Photosynthesis Dependency: Require sunlight and water for sustenance, interfering with concentration. Suffer disadvantage on Observation and Intellect checks unless you spend 2 hours in sunlight.
    3. Nature Vulnerability: Your plant-like nature makes you susceptible to fire and ice, suffering disadvantage on actions in extreme heat or cold.

      4. Upon completion of the initial stages: Gain Plant Adaptation: Immunity to poisons and diseases, no need to eat as long as you get 1 hour of sunlight. Heal 1 wound per hour and injuries every 3 hours, but you cannot take long rests.

      Greater Progression

      Remove previous disadvantages and unlock the following features in any order:

    4. Thermotolerance: Resist extreme environments, gaining +5 Armor and +5 Wounds.
    5. Vine Grasp: Extend roots to ensnare and drain energy from others. Deal [[1d6]] nature damage and heal for the same amount.
    6. Floral Minions: Create plant creatures under your control. As an action, create a PL 2 Root minion lasting 1 hour. Control minions up to your progression step.
    7. Barkform: Transform into a stationary tree. Gain +20 Armor but have the pinned and exhaustion conditions.
    8. Verdant Command: Control plant creatures weaker than you unless dominated by a powerful creature.
    9. Seed Bomb: Launch a cluster of seeds, dealing [[1d4]] piercing damage. Sprouting seeds may create [[1d4]] seedlings acting under your command.

    Final Step - Ascension to the Verdant Sovereign

    At the height of your transformation, you become a Verdant Sovereign:

    • Regain 3 wounds at the start of your turn.
    • Control any plant creature you can see unless dominated by a powerful entity.
    • Gain immunity to nature damage.
    • If you take the death condition, you become a seed pod for [[1d6]] weeks. If intact, you regenerate with full wounds and injuries.
    Resonance Sickness

    Resonance Sickness

    A rare affliction caused by exposure to high amounts of Vozian energy or interactions with Vozian creatures. This sickness disrupts the body and mind, creating a dangerous and transformative condition.

    Disease Progression

    1. Sonic Disruption: A constant disorienting hum impairs cognitive abilities. Disadvantage on all mental checks (Intellect, Observation, Influence).
    2. Vibratory Strain: The hum intensifies, causing slight bodily vibrations. Disadvantage on all physical checks (Agility, Crafting, Stealth, Strength, Survival).
    3. Energetic Vulnerability: Sensitivity to Vozian energies makes you susceptible to their effects. Disadvantage on actions near Vozian energy or artifacts.

      4. Upon completion: Gain Vozian Adaptation: Immunity to disease, +2 to all checks, and a glowing skin effect visible to those with Vozian Sight.

      Greater Progression

      Remove previous disadvantages and unlock these features in any order:

    4. Infernal Conjuring: Summon minor demons from the Abyss. Control a number of demons equal to your progression step.
    5. Soul Siphoning: Drain life from others to empower yourself. Deal [[1d6]] Vozian damage and heal for the same amount.
    6. Pact of Deception: Form contracts binding creatures to your will in exchange for boons.
    7. Dreadful Presence: Intimidate enemies within short range. Failures result in a cognitive condition of your choice.
    8. Vozo's Grasp: Mark a creature to always know its location and gain a +5 to checks against it.
    9. Vozian Augmentation: Gain either Wings (30ft fly speed) or Chitinous Armor (+6 armor, regenerates 1 per minute).

    Final Step - Ascension to the Resonant Overlord

    At the height of the sickness, transform into a Resonant Overlord:

    • +6 bonus to all physical checks and +3 to all damage.
    • Control can be taken by anyone who knows your true name.
    • If reduced to 0 wounds, become a Vozian energy core for [[1d6]] weeks. Regain consciousness and reform your body from nearby materials if the core is intact.
    Yeeshnu's Plague

    Yeeshnu's Plague

    Yeeshnu, the god of plague and pestilence, spreads its influence through disease. Yeeshnu's Plague is a horrifying affliction contracted from bites of vile creatures and spreads through filth and decay.

    Infection

    Following a bite from a rat or similar sewer-dwelling creature, the victim must make a Survival check. On failure, symptoms manifest after [[1d4]] days.

    Disease Progression

    1. Unseen Malady: The victim cannot recover wounds or injuries from long rests. Gain a resource called plague points, adding one each day.
    2. Chosen of Yeeshnu: Plague points exceed total wounds, and the victim transforms into a Chosen of Yeeshnu. Gain dark spellcasting effects and secrete a repugnant pus.
    3. Plague Spreader: Spread the plague to others. Reaching 20 plague points makes you a vessel for Yeeshnu's will.

      4. Upon completion: Gain Blessings of Yeeshnu: Immunity to disease, start with 3 plague points, and unlock plague-based abilities.

      Greater Progression

      Remove disadvantages and unlock these abilities in any order:

    4. Herald of Plague: Infect and control creatures within 5 ft for 1 minute using a plague point. Successful saves grant immunity.
    5. Pus Armor: Spend 1 plague point to gain [[3d6]] armor for 1 minute.
    6. Summon Plaguelings: Spend 1 plague point to summon [[2d4]] plaguelings that attack non-diseased creatures.
    7. Plague Bolt: Cast a bolt of disease. Targets must make a Survival check or take [[1d10]] necrotic damage.
    8. Miasma of Yeeshnu: Create a cloud of pestilence. Targets in the cloud must make a Survival check or take [[1d4]] toxic damage and risk infection.
    9. Undying Plague: If you take the death condition, make a Survival check to remove it for 1 minute. Afterward, your body dissolves into plague, giving your soul to Yeeshnu.

    Final Step - Yeeshnu's Herald

    As the ultimate manifestation of Yeeshnu's will:

    • Command all lesser plague bearers and plague-ridden creatures.
    • Spread the plague through your presence.
    • Communicate directly with Yeeshnu for missions and guidance.
    • Draw the attention of forces of good, including clerics and paladins, who see you as a significant threat.
