Equipment

In the diverse world of Shek, equipment is more than just tools; it's an extension of a hero's identity and story. This section offers a variety of equipment options, each with its own history and value. As you prepare for your journey, choose gear that enhances your character's story and prepares them for the challenges of Shek.

Basic Weaponry

Weapons in Shek range from traditional to exotic, classified into two main types based on the required physical attributes: Strength and Agility. This guide simplifies understanding these weapons and their broader applications beyond combat.

Weapon Classification and Mechanics

In the vast world of Shek, weapons are classified into two foundational categories based on the attributes they rely upon:

  • Strength-Based Weapons: Heavy and powerful weapons like hammers and broadswords, requiring brute force for impact.
  • Agility-Based Weapons: Lightweight weapons like daggers, rapiers, and bows, relying on finesse and precision.
  • Standard Weapons: Basic and simple weapons that can be wielded with either Strength or Agility.

Weapon Properties

  • Light: Lightweight and easy to wield, allowing for quick and fluid movements. Can be dual-wielded or used as an Off-Action.
  • Free Hands: Your hands are free and not occupied by the weapon.
  • Attached: This weapon is attached and cannot be disarmed.
  • Thrown: Can be used in melee or thrown for ranged attacks with short range.
  • Range: Weapons operate within specific ranges:
    • Melee: 5ft
    • Reach: 10ft
    • Short: Under 60ft
    • Medium: 60-220ft
    • Long: 220-600ft

    Optional rule: Attacking over half the range imposes disadvantage.

  • Hefty: Excessively heavy. Carrying more than two Hefty items causes the Pinned condition.
  • Reload: Requires reloading using an action, half-action, off-action, or movement.
  • Explosive: Causes powerful explosions that deal damage to all within range.
  • Hidden: Designed to be concealed, making detection difficult.
  • Mechanical: Can be disrupted, jammed, or broken.
Damage & Damage Types

In the world of Shek, numerous damage types exist, each with unique properties and effects. When a character rolls a success+ or greater with a weapon or spell, they have the opportunity to roll on the associated damage table, leading to a variety of outcomes. Here's an overview of the damage types and their potential interactions with armor, wounds, and injuries: These are organized by physical, elemental, and Realm Damage types. These are in each category not because of the amount of damage they do but what conditions and effects they cause on their damage tables. physical damage will often just deal additional damage while Realm Damage can cause immediate injuries or banish creatures to alternate planes momentarily. If an ability, weapon, gear, spell or whatever says it deals physical damage, it means a physical damage type of your choice. Same thing if it says elemental or realm. If you are given a choice at the time of creation of an item that usually means that item is locked into that damage type, unless you and the narrator determine that it is a shifting item or if it says so in the description. A weapon can only have one type of damage. If you have abilities that add damage to a weapon, spell or ability then it adds that on and doesn't change the type unless otherwise specified. A glyph can change the type of damage on a weapon. When you make a check with multiple successes then the weapon‚Äôs damage type is used, not the additional damage types.

Physical Damage

Water, blood, oil, and other liquids can overwhelm and suffocate. Fluid damage includes the crushing force of tidal waves, the searing burn of boiling liquids, and the asphyxiation of drowning or being engulfed by fluids.

Harnessing the primal forces of the natural world, nature damage draws upon plants, animals, and the elements. It can manifest as thorny vines, swarming insects, or the relentless force of wind and rain, often bringing disease and decay to those who fall victim.

It's vital to remember that different weapons come with distinct damage tables, so strategic weapon selection can offer unique advantages in combat.

    Armor

    When adventuring, armor is your primary line of defense. It's essential to ally with a blacksmith who can repair and upgrade your gear. In combat, armor absorbs hits before they affect your health. Remember, armor is not destroyed when its protection points are depleted; it simply can't offer protection until repaired.

    Armor also shields against poison, as venom requires direct contact with the skin to be effective. Be cautious, as wearing too much heavy gear can impose the 'pinned' condition, hindering your movement.

    Repair Methods

    • Quick Repair: Characters must succeed in a Crafting check once per hour to repair [[1d6]] + Crafting points of armor integrity. This represents quick fixes using whatever materials are at hand.
    • Standard Repair: Utilizing a workbench, characters can repair armor, restoring half the total armor value + Crafting points per successful check per hour. This involves physical repairs like patching holes or replacing segments.
    • Magical Repair: Using spell points, repair [[1d6]] per spell point. This is an action requiring 1 spell point.

    Bulk Items

    Bulk represents the weight and space your gear occupies, from negligible trinkets to burdensome hauls. It's not just about weight but also the size and how cumbersome an item is. Managing your bulk is crucial for maintaining agility and avoiding encumbrance.

    For example, a watch has 0 Bulk because it can be worn and forgotten about. Meanwhile, an empty barrel may be light but is awkward to carry, giving it a Bulk of 5.

    Bulk Classifications

    Bulk Description Examples
    0 Small/wearable items Watch, ring, eyeglasses
    1 Backpack-sized items Clothes, flask, small tools
    5 Large items Armor, barrel, large pot
    10 Heavy items Furniture, small canoe
    15 Very heavy items Piano, cart
    20 Extremely heavy items Small car, heavy safe

    Bulk Penalties

    • Bulk exceeds Strength: Over-encumbered (Pinned condition).
    • Bulk > 2x Strength: Over-encumbered + Exhausted condition.
    • Worn items: Worn items have no bulk.
    • Bundles: Bundles reduce the average bulk of items.
    • Backpacks: Reduce total bulk carried.

    Mounted Combat

    Mounted combat stands as a thrilling fusion of tradition and innovation. Knights and daredevils alike take to the streets and skies, not on horses, but on mechanized steeds and mystical creatures. Here, a well-timed cavalry charge might be led by a line of sleek motorcycles, while Pegasus-mounted aces could duel among the skyscrapers. In this era, the mounted warrior's valor is matched only by the speed of their mount, be it flesh or steel, feathered or geared.

    Rules for Mounted Combat

    • Mount Movement: The base speed for beasts of burden such as horses is 60 ft. Speed for other creatures used as mounts will be determined based on their type and size.
    • Target Selection: Enemies may choose to direct their attacks at either you or your mount.
    • Dismounting & Mounting: Exiting your mount requires either the expenditure of 20 ft of movement or an action, and the same for mounting a creature or vehicle.
    • Mount Abilities: Different types of mounts provide various abilities and combat options. For example:
      • Warhorse: Might grant a charge ability that allows for a powerful attack after moving a certain distance.
      • Flying Mount: Could offer aerial movement options, but with limitations on how long they can stay aloft or how they maneuver.
      • Aquatic Mount: May allow for swift underwater movement and possibly an evasion bonus due to the fluid environment.

    Glyphs

    Glyphs

    Glyphs are powerful symbols infused with magic, capable of granting weapons, armor, and even characters with remarkable abilities and enhancements. Through glyphs, a simple piece of gear can become a legendary artifact, and a character may access abilities that significantly alter their role and capabilities within the game.

    Crafting and Application

    • Implementation: Crafting and applying glyphs require specific materials and a suitable crafting station, such as an enchanting table or magical lab. The rarity of materials and quality of the station can affect the outcome.
    • Crafting Process: Crafters must perform Crafting, Intellect, Spirit, or other related checks. The complexity of the glyph determines the number of successes required.
    • Application Process: Applying a glyph involves a similar check to integrate the glyph's magical essence fully. Each step takes one hour, with more complex glyphs requiring additional time.
    • Risk of Failure: Failed crafting or application checks can damage or destroy the item, with consequences scaling based on the severity of the failure.

    Glyph Tiers

    ‚ú® Basic Glyphs

    Enhance damage, armor, or checks, ideal for incremental improvements to gear.

    Requirements: 1 check to craft, 1 to apply; each step takes an hour and can be done with minimal equipment.

    • Glyph of Fortification: Increases armor by +2.
    • Glyph of Sharpness: Adds +1 to damage rolls on bladed weapons.

    ‚öîÔ∏è Advanced Glyphs

    Introduce new mechanics like special attacks, defensive maneuvers, or unique effects.

    Requirements: 2 checks to craft and apply; requires a proper workstation and additional materials or assistance.

    • Glyph of Rebounding: Reflects half of damage back to the attacker as an off-action (reaction).
    • Glyph of Swiftness: Increases movement speed by +20ft when applied to footwear.
    • Glyph of Silence: Silences weapons or armor, ideal for stealthy characters.

    üåü Legendary Glyphs

    Provide progression systems and grant game-changing abilities or major character transformations.

    Requirements: 3 checks to craft and apply; requires a powerful workstation and a team effort.

    • Glyph of Transformation: Allows the character to shift into a specific creature, gaining its abilities temporarily.
    • Glyph of Time Manipulation: once per long rest, take 2 actions on your turn.
    • Glyph of Elemental Mastery: Grants control over a specific element for strategic advantages.
