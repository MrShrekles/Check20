          Action Types

          • Move: Up to your Speed
          • Action: The primary activity you perform, like attacking or casting a spell. You get one action per turn, though certain abilities may offer more.
          • Half-Action: Instead of a single action, take 2 half-actions. These are things like picking things up or Stances
          • Off-Action: Can be used on your turn or after. Most used for Provoke attacks or light weapon strikes
          • Non-Action: Talk, switch items, other
          • Finisher: Eliminate locked or submitted foes

          Movement

          • Difficult Terrain: Halved speed
          • Jump: 2ft × Agiltiy or Strength
          • Climb/Swim: Counts as difficult terrain
          • Prone: Disadv on range attacks; adv for melee attacks against you. Stand costs 15ft
          • Moving Past Creatures: Can only move past smaller creature
          • Dash: Double distance (Action)
          • Fall: 5 dmg per 10ft
          • Extreme Hazard: 10 dmg/sec
          • Burrowing: Are unseen. Can't target nor breathe
          • Teleport: unoccupied space, no opportunity attacks.

          Non-Actions

          • Switch Weapons: Drop a weapon and draw another.
          • Stress Push: Take 4 stress damage to gain a +3 bonus on a low roll.
          • Roleplay: Briefly communicate or give orders without consequence.

          Special

          • Called Shot: −10 for condition
          • Surprise: additional action
          • Cover: Disadv. to be hit
          • Full Cover: Cannot be targetted without line of sight

          Actions

          • Attack: Make a melee or ranged attack. Unarmed deal 1 dmg
          • Grab or Hold Contested Physical Check (Strength Default)
          • Cast Spell: Requires seeing and perceiving the target, and having free hands.
          • Administer Potion: Use an action to give a potion to a downed ally.
          • Stealth: Attempt to hide for advantage on the next attack.

          Half-Actions

          • Stances: Choose a stance to remain in until your next turn.
          • Disarm: Make a contested Strength or Agility check to disarm a creature.
          • Help: Give a creature +1 to a check
          • Pick up an Item: Picking up an item is a Half-Action.
          • Use an Object: Interact with objects like levers or buttons.
          • Disengage: Move out of a creature's range without provoking opportunity attacks.

          Stances

          • Advantage Stance: Gain advantage on your next attack.
          • Disadvantage Stance: Give disadvantage to enemies attacking you or allies in range.
          • Guard Stance: When a creature attacks you, Provoke without using an Off-Action once. On all failed attacks, you may also Provoke at disadvantage.

          Off-Actions

          • Provoke: Make an attack against a creature that has missed an attack directly targeting you, or against a creature that willingly leaves your melee weapon range.
          • Block: As an Off-Action, reduce incoming damage by half if you have Armor from a shield
          • Demoralize: Make a contested Influence check against an intelligent creature. On a success, the target gains the Fear condition.

          Size Scale

          • Tiny: < 1 ft – pixies, potions
          • Small: 1–4 ft; goblins
          • Medium: 5–7 ft; humans
          • Large: 8–15 ft; ogres
          • Giant: 15–25 ft; siege equipment
          • Monolithic: 25–100 ft; building

          Press On

          After a combat encounter, characters may Press On to recover:

          • 1 class resource and 1 spell point
          • Armor repair: [[1d6]] + Crafting
          • Healing: [[1d6]] + Survival
          • Remove all conditions and effects lasting 1 minute or less

          Press On may be used twice per day.

          Long Rest

          Requires 10 hours of uninterrupted downtime in a safe environment. Restores:

          • All wounds
          • All class resources and spell points
          • Armor repaired to at least half
          • Remove all conditions lasting one day or less

          Can only be taken once per day.

          Check Result Outcomes

          • 1–4: Fail+ Provoke @Adv
          • 5–9: Fail: Provoke
          • 10–14: Fail: Provoke @Dis
          • 15–19: Success
          • 20–24: Success + — 1 roll
          • 25–29: Success ++ — 2 rolls
          • 30–34: Success +++ — 3 rolls
