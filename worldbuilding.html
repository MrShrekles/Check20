Narrator Guide

As a narrator, you are the architect of the story and world. This guide includes advice on setting up challenges, balancing mechanics, and facilitating immersive gameplay.

Enemies and NPCs

The Power Level (PL) system provides a simple and effective way to create and manage monsters, enemies, and NPCs. This system allows narrators to quickly generate creatures with appropriate stats for any encounter, ensuring balance and flexibility.

Basic Mechanics

Basic Mechanics

  • Standard Check: Roll 1d6 + Power Level (PL) for all checks. Specific bonuses may apply for special creatures.
  • Standard Damage: Roll 1d6 + PL for damage, choosing a logical type based on the creature.
  • Capacity: Capacity is a number of reactions, condition removals, special actions, or plot moments for combat. If this is a traveling NPC or vehicle, these recover after 1 on Press On and all on a Long Rest.
  • HP: Instead of Armor and Wounds, npcs and monsters have HP which is a number of points of damage they can take before becoming dead. Monsters do not take the Injured condition, but you could allow an important NPC to have the INjured condiiton
  • Number of Attacks: A monster could have a numnber of attacks equal to Capacity divided by 3 (minimum of 1), rounded down

Abilities
Defensive Abilities
  • Hardened Carapace (Passive): Increases armor by 10 x PL.
  • Counterstrike (Reaction): On being attacked, the monster can make an immediate attack against the aggressor, spending a capacity point.
  • Regeneration (Special Ability): Regains health 1d(PL) or (PL)d4+ at the start of its turn.
Utility Abilities
  • Terrifying Roar (Action): Inflicts the Fear condition on enemies within range.
  • Burrow (Half-Action): Allows movement underground to avoid attacks and emerge for an unexpected strike.
  • Summon Minions (Action): Summons lesser creatures to aid in combat (e.g., 1d4, 2d6, or 3d8 based on PL).
Special Attacks
  • Grapple (Action): Attempts to seize and immobilize an opponent.
  • Swallow Whole (Action): Consumes a smaller creature, dealing internal and acid damage.
  • Blast Wave (Action): Releases a shockwave that damages and knocks back all nearby foes.
Reaction Abilities
  • Retaliate (Reaction): Strikes back at an attacker when hit by a close-range attack.
  • Absorb Spell (Reaction): Absorbs an incoming spell, converting it into health or energy at the cost of a capacity point.

Locks & Traps

  • Mundane Lock & Physical Key: A standard lock that requires a traditional Physical Key. It can range from basic padlocks to more complex tumbler mechanisms. These locks are susceptible to lockpicking or brute force.
  • Blood Lock & Blood Key: A Blood Lock requires the touch of blood to open. Depending on the difficulty, it might require the blood of a specific creature, a creature type, or just any generic blood. A Blood Key often takes the form of a dagger-like object that requires a cut to activate, channeling the essence of the wielder into the lock.
  • Soul Lock & Soul Key: A Soul Lock remains closed until touched by a specific soul linked to it. In more challenging versions, a soul must be sacrificed for the lock to open. The corresponding Soul Key may manifest as an enchanted crystal or token infused with the required soul or spiritual essence.
  • Trap Rune Lock & Rune Key: A Trap Rune Lock detonates when tampered with, layering protective runes that require an Arcane Rune Key to disarm. The key itself is often a magical glyph or talisman that matches the runes’ inscription.
  • Construct Mimic Lock & Password: The Construct Mimic Lock is a sentient mechanism resembling a lock but requiring a specific password to deactivate. The lock may interact with those attempting to open it, even bargaining or testing them for entry.
  • Illusionary Lock & Perception: An Illusionary Lock appears real but is not truly locked. Often, it hides a secondary trap, tricking the unwary into ignoring other dangers. Observant individuals or those skilled in perception can identify it for what it is.
  • Tech Lock & Code Key: A Tech Lock incorporates advanced mechanisms such as keypads, fingerprint readers, or voice-activated commands. These locks require a Code Key, which might be a passphrase, biometric data, or a specific tool like a data chip.
  • Basic Magic Lock & Spell Key: This lock relies on simple magical energy and requires a basic incantation or a low-power spell to unlock. The Spell Key might be a single word of power or a specific hand motion.
  • Celestial Lock & Star Key: A Celestial Lock glimmers with divine light or cosmic energy. It can only be opened with a Star Key, which might take the form of a constellation map, a blessed object, or a mote of starlight.
  • Chaos Lock & Entropy Key: A swirling, unpredictable Chaos Lock shifts and changes form, preventing easy access. The Entropy Key is equally erratic, requiring a random or illogical approach to use properly.
  • Chronomancy Lock & Temporal Key: A Chronomancy Lock is bound by time itself, only accessible at specific moments or when manipulated with a Temporal Key, such as a clockwork device or a relic attuned to time magic.
  • Crystal Lock & Resonance Key: Crafted from shimmering crystal, the Crystal Lock resonates with specific frequencies. A Resonance Key, often a crystal shard or tuning fork, must match the lock's frequency to open it.
  • Dragon Lock & Wyrm Key: Guarded by ancient draconic magic, the Dragon Lock often requires the breath or blood of a dragon to unlock. A Wyrm Key might be a scale, a claw, or a sigil carved in the language of dragons.
  • Elemental Lock & Element Key: The Elemental Lock binds its magic to one of the primal forces (fire, water, air, or earth). The Element Key could be a manifestation of the element, such as a flame, a vial of water, or a stone from a sacred place.
  • Nature Lock & Grove Key: A Nature Lock thrives with vines, wood, or blooming flowers. It requires a Grove Key, such as a sacred leaf, a druid's token, or a whispered prayer to the forest spirits.
  • Vozian Lock & Infernal Key: A dark and malevolent Vozian Lock pulses with infernal energy. It requires an Infernal Key, often a cursed artifact or a pact with an evil entity, to open.

Trap Power Levels (PL)

Trap PL is a modular system that allows narrators to scale traps for their desired level of danger or challenge. Each PL adds to the damage, range, or complexity of a trap. Use the following guidelines to adapt traps:

  • Damage: Traps deal 1d6 damage per PL (e.g., a PL 2 trap deals 2d6 damage).
  • Range: Trap effects scale by area or target range. A PL 1 trap has a range of 5 ft, increasing by +5 ft per PL. For example, a PL 10 trap would affect a 50 ft radius.
  • +Table: Every 3 PL adds a roll on the +Table for the damage type of the trap. For example, a PL 3 trap would act similarly to a Success+ on a weapon attack, adding effects like conditions or bonus damage.
  • Disarm: Traps can typically be disarmed with either a Physical check (e.g., Agility or Strength) or a Mental check (e.g., Intellect or Spirit), depending on the trap type.

Generic Trap Types

Below are common trap concepts for narrators to customize. Apply damage types, conditions, or thematic elements as needed.

  • Dart Trap: A hidden mechanism fires a projectile when triggered. Damage type can be chosen by the narrator (e.g., Toxic, Piercing, Fire).
  • Rune Trap: Magical symbols activate when disturbed, unleashing effects like explosions, curses, or elemental damage. Narrators can define the damage type (e.g., Fire, Lightning, or Psychic).
  • Trap Door: A concealed mechanism drops the target into a pit or hazard. Damage type depends on the environment (e.g., Impact, Spikes, or Lava).
  • Gas Trap: Releases a harmful cloud when triggered. Narrators can specify conditions (e.g., Coughing, Poisoned) and damage type (e.g., Toxic or Eclipse).
  • Net Trap: Launches a net to immobilize targets, applying the Pinned condition. Narrators can add enhancements like barbs (Piercing damage) or elemental effects.
  • Illusion Trap: Creates a deceptive or disorienting visual or auditory effect. Can impose conditions like Blind, Confused, or Fear.
  • Explosive Trap: Triggers an area-of-effect blast. Damage type can range from Fire to Thunder or even Vozian for infernal-themed traps.

Trap Customization Ideas

  • Conditions: Add conditions like Coughing, Prone, or Blinded to enhance the trap’s complexity.
  • Triggers: Traps can be triggered by pressure plates, tripwires, magical detection, or proximity.
  • Themes: Adjust the theme of traps to fit your setting (e.g., jungle-themed vine traps, steampunk clockwork mechanisms, or infernal traps with cursed effects).
