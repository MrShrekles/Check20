Reference
What to do on your turn
  • Move: Move any distance up to your speed during your turn.
  • Action: Perform main abilities like attack or cast a spell.
  • Half-Action: Perform two minor actions instead of 1 action, like switching stances.
  • Off- Action: Can be an additional attack with a light weapon or a provoke to a missed attack.
  • Non-Action: Quick tasks like talking that require minimal effort.
  • Finisher: Eliminate defenseless opponents that are locked from combat.

    • Positioning in Combat

  • Cover disadvantage to hit, full cover cannot be targeted.
  • Range: Melee, reach(10ft), short(under 50ft), medium(50-200), and long(200+) distances.
Types of Movement
  • Regular: Move up to your speed.
  • Dash Move double distance as an action.
  • Jumping: Leap distances based on toughness (5ft per check, 2x with running start).
  • Climbing: Requires agility checks and can lead to falling.
  • Difficult Terrain/Swimming: Halves movement speed.
  • Prone Standing up uses 15ft movement.
  • Moving Past Creatures: Only allies or small enemies.
  • Falling: 5 impact damage per 10 ft
  • Extreme Conditions: Hazardous environments cause extreme damage (10 per second).
  • Burrowing: full cover while burrowed, cannot breath.
Actions
  • Attack: Make a combat attack.
  • Unarmed Strike: Deal minor damage without weapons.
  • Grab or Hold: Grapple opponents, contested Strength or Agility checks.
  • Stealth: Conceal presence for strategic advantage.
  • Cast a Spell: Perform magical actions with specific requirements.
  • Dash Move double distance as an action.
  • Administer a Potion Heal or aid allies.
Half-Actions & Stances

    Stances:

    As a half-action, adopt battle stances for various combat benefits. They start at the end of your turn and last until the start of your next turn.
    • Give your next attack advantage
    • Give disadvantage on attack rolls against you and allies within your melee range.
    • When a creature attacks you, Provoke without using an Off-Action once. On all failed attacks, you may also Provoke at disadvantage.

    Half Actions:

  • Pick up an Item: Retrieve items during combat.
  • Use an Object: Interact with objects in the environment.
  • Escape: Break free from restraints or remove pinned conditon.
  • Disarm: Attempt to remove an opponent's weapon.
  • Help: Assist one creature within reach range for advantage on a check, only one creature can help at a time.
  • Disengage: Retreat without provoking opportunity attacks.
  • Shove: Make an opposed Strength or Agility check to knock a creature prone.
  • Taunt: force enemies that can hear you to make a check, on failures they have to target you on their next turn.
Off Actions
  • Provoke: Strike at enemies who drop their guard.
  • Drink Something: Quickly consume something for benefits.
  • Give an attacking creature within melee range disadvantage on their attack on a successful check (Agility, Influence, Luck, Stealth, Strength or observation to try and disrupt)
  • As an Off-Action, reduce incoming damage by half if you have Armor from a shield.
  • As an off action once per creature, make a Influence (Intimidate) and on a success the enemy must make a morale check, fleeing on a failure.
Non-Actions
  • This is an action that doesnt require effort. However you are usually limited to 1 non-action, therefore cannot switch weapons multiple times and try to limit roleplay to one instance
  • Take 4 points of stress damage in exchange for a boost of up to 3 points on any check scoring under 15 to 15.
  • Switch Weapons: Change armaments during combat.
  • Roleplay: Engage in brief in-character exchanges.
  • Shouting Orders: Command allies or intimidate enemies.
Special Actions
  • Called Shots: take -10 to a check or an attack check to cause a condition.
  • Surprise Turns: Gain an extra action under certain conditions.
  • Targeting: Engage enemies using various senses, this is part of an action.
  • Cover disadvantage to hit, full cover cannot be targeted. Use environmental cover for protection. This would be part of movement.
Size
  • Tiny:Below 1 ft – Examples: Insects, small birds, coins, potion bottles.
  • Small:1-4 ft – Examples: Cats, small dogs, scroll cases, books.
  • Medium:5-7 ft – Examples: Humans, elves, armor sets, single weapons.
  • Large:8-15 ft – Examples: Ogres, bears, large shields, small boats.
  • Giant:15-25 ft – Examples: Elephants, siege towers, large trees.
  • Monolithic:25-100 ft – Examples: Large statues, grand castles, airships.
  • Enviromental:100-300 ft – Examples: Mountains, massive ancient trees, celestial landmarks.

Capacity Actions

Capacity represents a creature’s reserve of extra effort, provokes, or dramatic maneuvers. It does not replace their normal Action on their turn. Instead, Capacity can be spent to react to events outside their turn or to take an additional Action on their turn

Make an additional attack immediately.

1 Capacity

End one condition currently affecting this creature.

1 Capacity

Move without provoking opportunity attacks.

1 Capacity

Reduce incoming damage by half.

1 Capacity

Add or remove an environmental element like cover, difficult terrain, hazards, or LoS blockers within Short range.

2 Capacity

Attempt to escape and kick off a chase. Party needs successes equal to Capacity spent to stop the escape.

Crafting

Crafting Overview

Crafting allows players to collaboratively create custom items, requiring specific skills, materials, and the right workbench. Crafted items gain enhanced effects based on the quality achieved during crafting.

Crafting an item requires downtime or a long rest with a suitable workbench. Each participant at a workstation contributes a skill check; total successes determine the quality.

Quality
Quality Type Total Successes Description
Poor Quality 3 or fewer Basic or flawed version of the item.
Good Quality 4 to 6 Reliable and functional item.
Great Quality 7 to 10 Enhanced item with minor bonuses.
Legendary Quality 10+ (requires Legendary Workbench) Extraordinary quality with high bonuses.
