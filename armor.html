Armor

When adventuring, armor is your primary line of defense. It's essential to ally with a blacksmith who can repair and upgrade your gear. In combat, armor absorbs hits before they affect your health. Remember, armor is not destroyed when its protection points are depleted; it simply can't offer protection until repaired.

Armor also shields against poison, as venom requires direct contact with the skin to be effective. Be cautious, as wearing too much heavy gear can impose the 'pinned' condition, hindering your movement.

Types of Mundane Armor

Armor Penalties: If a check's modifier is negative, it is considered a penalty. Features that negate penalties can remove these effects.

Magic Penalty: This is in reference to your spellcasting check, whatever it might be for your Class, Path, Talent, and so on

Shield Bash

Use your shield as a weapon. Make a Strength check as an off-action to deal [[1d4]] impact damage to an enemy.

Repair Methods

  • Quick Repair: Characters must succeed in a Crafting check once per hour to repair [[1d6]] + Crafting points of armor integrity. This represents quick fixes using whatever materials are at hand.
  • Standard Repair: Utilizing a workbench, characters can repair armor, restoring half the total armor value + Crafting points per successful check per hour. This involves physical repairs like patching holes or replacing segments.
  • Magical Repair: Using spell points, repair [[1d6]] per spell point. This is an action requiring 1 spell point.
