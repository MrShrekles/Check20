About the Rank

Divine Ranks

In Shek, Rank is a measure of cosmic power. The lower the number, the more fundamental and dangerous the being. Rank is both a place in the hierarchy and a power value.

Rank 0 belongs only to the Seven, the universal truths that shape time, matter, death, thought, and other constants. They are not gods, they are the rules the universe runs on, and cannot be fought or destroyed.

Ranks 1–50 cover planetary titans and primal forces (such as Tala or Khalas). These beings shape continents, climates, and ages. Mortals do not defeat them. At best, mortals survive their moods.

Ranks 50–100 are the greater and lesser gods, arch spirits, and mythic progenitors. They rule realms, pantheons, and great cycles. A whole campaign can revolve around resisting or confronting a single being in this band.

Ranks 100–150 are powerful but limited entities: high demigods, ancient dragons, fallen celestials, great demons, and world scale monsters. This is where a dedicated group of heroes can realistically defeat a “god level” foe if the entire story is built around that goal.

Ranks 150–200 mark legendary beings, ascended heroes, regional guardian spirits, and unique monsters. Their fall can change kingdoms or eras, but not the structure of the world itself.

Below this scale are ordinary mortals and monsters. They do not register as divine, but through story, sacrifice, and strange bargains, they can still reach upward and wound the ranks above them.
