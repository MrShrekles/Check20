How to Make a Character

  1. Choose a Class: Begin by selecting a class that will define your character's core abilities.
  2. Select a Path & Talents of your Class: Refine your character's abilities and potential growth with a path and associated talents.
  3. Class Path Progression: Reflects the overall growth in a character's chosen class. It's linear and sequential, with each step unlocking new class-specific abilities. At 1, 3, 6, and 9 gain +3 points to put into your checks to a maximum of 15.
  4. Talent Progression: Focuses on specialized skills or unique capabilities within a class. Steps within a Talent can be chosen and advanced in any order, allowing for customization and you gain +1 per talent chosen.
  5. Determine your species: Choose a species type for your character and apply the corresponding features to your sheet.
  6. Fill Out Checks (Stats): Finalize your character's stats, ensuring you've covered all essential attributes.
    • You have 20 points to put into your checks with a maximum of 10.
    • It's recommended that you put a 10, 5, 3, and 2 with some slight variations.
  7. Roll or Pick from each of these tables:
    • Motivation: Determine their main goals.
    • Additional Equipment: A pack of equipment.
    • Trinket: Trinkets are a fun item that can spark ideas and backstory about your character.
  8. Personalize Your Character:
    • Name: Reflect your character's spirit.
    • Size: Default is medium, but your species options may specify height.
    • Gender: Define your character's pronouns.
    • Age: Decide if they're young or old.
    • Diet: Define their eating habits.
    • Speed: Base speed is 30ft, + 5ft per 2 points of agility.
    • Gold: Start with 1d100 + 50 gp.

Classes

  • Tank: The frontline defender with high health and defensive skills. Specializes in absorbing damage.
  • Professional: The versatile problem-solver, skilled in areas like lock-picking and engineering.
  • Support: The medic, focused on healing allies through magic or herbalism.
  • Merchant: The master of trade, specializing in negotiation, crafting, and commerce.
  • Mage: A spellcaster with a wide range of magical abilities, from offense to healing.

Paths & Talents

Path: Represents your character’s progression and growth in power. Gaining new skills and enhancing existing ones shapes your character’s journey.

Talents: Customizable skills and abilities that add unique aspects to your character. These allow for personalized development and specialization.

Experience & Progression

  • Experience Points (XP): Earned through adventures, XP is used to improve your character.
  • Progression: Each class path has 10 steps, requiring 20 XP per step to advance.
  • Class and Talent Progression:
    • Path progression is linear, unlocking new class abilities.
    • Talent progression allows players to choose from a pool of skills.
  • Spending XP: Use XP to enhance class features, talents, or unique aspects of your character.
Tank

As a Tank, your character embodies the very essence of strength, resilience, and unyielding fortitude. You are the shield that safeguards your allies, the bulwark that stands firm in the face of the enemy onslaught. With every swing of your weapon, you command the battlefield, drawing the attention of foes and absorbing their blows that would otherwise kill your companions and many other creatures as well. Your unmatched stamina and formidable armor make you a veritable living fortress, capable of withstanding onslaughts that would obliterate the less hardy.

However, the Tank's might in combat comes with a trade-off. In situations that require finesse, diplomacy, or arcane knowledge, you may find yourself akin to a fish out of water. While your strength and endurance are beyond question, tasks that involve subtlety, stealth, or intricate problem-solving might present a challenge. It's in these moments that you'll need to rely on your allies, just as they depend on your unwavering protection in battle. As a Tank, your role is not to master every situation, but to provide a rock-solid foundation from which your group can confront any danger. Always remember, true strength comes not just from physical power, but from knowing your limits and trusting in your comrades.

Talent

Tank Talents are your arsenal of survival and defense. They define your unique approach to absorbing damage and shielding allies. Whether it's an unyielding stance or a protective maneuver, these Talents forge your identity as the group's bastion.

Professional

The Professional is a seasoned individual, having faced the tumultuous tides of time and emerging as a beacon of resilience and expertise. Whether they've survived the brutal trenches of war or navigated the intricate politics of the big city, their experiences have crafted them into unparalleled specialists in their fields. They are the ex-military tacticians employing strategies in the boardroom, the seasoned detectives with an uncanny ability to read a crime scene, or the old-time engineers who've seen the rise of the modern age. Their scars, both visible and hidden, are a testament to their enduring spirit, their stories etched in every line on their face.

A Professional is characterized by their unwavering determination, their vast experience making them adaptable and invaluable. They've seen the world change, and they've changed with it, but their core – forged in the crucible of challenges – remains unyielding. They don't merely survive; they lead, mentor, and inspire.

Support

As the quintessential support specialist, this class is the embodiment of both the moral compass guiding their allies toward righteous victories and the sinner's wingman, offering redemption and support in their quest for atonement. This character thrives in the role of empowering and protecting their comrades, utilizing a keen understanding of both the battlefield and the human spirit.

Masters of both strategy and empathy, they harness a diverse array of abilities to shield friends from harm, enhance their fighting capabilities, and disrupt foes with tactical acumen. Beyond the tangible benefits of healing and bolstering defenses, they inspire greatness in their allies, lifting their spirits in the darkest times and offering guidance when moral dilemmas cloud the path forward.

Merchant

A shrewd and resourceful individual who knows how to make a profit no matter the circumstances. The Merchant is a master of trade and commerce, always looking for new opportunities to expand their business and increase their wealth. They are not content to simply rely on combat and adventuring to earn a living. Instead, they think beyond these traditional means and use their wits and cunning to get ahead. Whether it's looking for valuable items to sell or stealing from a dragon's hoard to further a political campaign, the Merchant of Adventure is always one step ahead.

Class Features

  • Starting Wealth: Start with 500 gp or 2d100 x5, and 4 Merchant Tokens that are powerful and magical representations of your wealth. These tokens can take any shape you wish, such as a coin, tattoo, or ring. You can spend these tokens on different business opportunities to possibly gain more tokens. If you show your token to another merchant or those in power, they will identify you as a Merchant, which could open doors but also mark you as a target. The Merchant Investments section offers samples of how you can spend your merchant tokens.
  • Social Network: In most well-populated areas, you have an ally, contact, or associate who might aid you with business endeavors, information, or finding the best deals. Make a social check of the narrator's choice (Influence, Luck, Spirit, or another based on the city's nature). On a success, you find your contact, who is connected in the town and can provide rumors, tips, and leads on whatever you might need. On a failure, you have an enemy in the area and should be on guard.
  • Appraisal: Quickly assess item values, identify if they're magical (but not if they're cursed), and find items for sale at a 10-30% discount when shopping, based on a successful Luck or Observation check.

Equipment

  • Armor: Choose one:
    • Light Armor (6 armor; Stealth +1, Agility +1)
    • Medium Armor (10 armor; Movement -10, Stealth -2, Magic -2, Agility +1)
  • Weapon: Choose one:
    • Standard Weapon (Agi or Str; 1 hand, Light; [[1d6]] Physical)
    • Revolver (Agi; medium range, 1 hand; [[2d6!]] Piercing)
    • Siege Crossbow (Str; 2 hand, Hefty, Medium Range, Mechanical, Reload; [[2d6]] Piercing)

Path

The Merchant's path is an entrepreneurial venture of commerce and craft. Progressing through the levels enhances your skills in negotiation, trade, and craftsmanship, evolving from a local trader to a renowned merchant prince.

Talent

Merchant Talents encapsulate your business acumen and craftsmanship. They represent your special flair for trade, be it a keen eye for valuable goods, a talent for persuasive bargaining, or an innovative approach to crafting and selling

Merchant Investments

Merchants can enhance their wealth by investing in businesses, which in turn generate more Merchant Tokens. The initial cost and potential returns of these businesses are set by specific guidelines based on their size. Here are some example businesses:

  • Small Business: A local bakery.
  • Medium Enterprise: A town's blacksmith.
  • Large Corporation: A citywide trade network.

Collaborate with your narrator to determine the specifics of your chosen business. Remember, running a business incurs operating costs. For instance, owning a stable implies expenses for horses, maintenance, etc. Though the system simplifies this aspect, players can further integrate their businesses into gameplay, using them as hubs or even hiring other party members.

Monthly Earnings Overview

Each in-game month, Merchants assess the performance of their ventures through an Earnings check. This determines the monthly profit. Factors influencing this check include the business's nature and scale, the game world's economic climate, and notable events of the past month.

For this check, opt for either a d20 or d100 roll. Multiply the result (as a percentage) by your business's potential monthly earnings to calculate your total token profit for that month.

Underworld Earnings Overview

For those Merchants embroiled in the underworld, monthly profits are a dance with danger. Every in-game month, perform an Underworld Earnings check to gauge the illicit gains. The outcome can be swayed by the type of operation, its reach, the region's law enforcement vigilance, and unexpected events, such as rival gang confrontations or heists gone wrong.

Choose to roll either a d20 or d100 for this check. Take the result (as a percentage) and multiply it by your venture's potential monthly earnings to deduce your total token haul for the month. But remember, higher rewards often come with greater risks.

Mage

As a mage, you are a weaver of magic and a master of mysterious forces. Your power is formidable, but it's also unpredictable, and you'll need a creative mind to control it. Unlike other classes, your spellcasting doesn't rely on a pre-set list of spells. Instead, you combine magic words to create your own unique spells. This path is not without its risks and challenges. Your magic can have both positive and negative outcomes, and it's crucial to learn how to manage these ups and downs. One of the crucial aspects of being a mage is the practice of recording and sharing your knowledge. Whether you pass on your wisdom through spellbooks or spiritual knowledge transfer, your experiences and discoveries become a legacy for future characters you play because they all share your soul.

An Excerpt from the Treatise on Arcane Oppositions by Professor Essley Yahir, the Esteemed Historian of Magic, Magic in all things, and Magical Theory, of the Stonetooth Academy of Magic the Gathering

“In the vast cosmos of magical energies, the origins of magic types are as diverse as the stars in the night sky. Each form of magic, while unique in its essence, finds its place in the grand tapestry of the arcane, often standing in stark opposition to another. This opposition, far from being a source of conflict, is a testament to the balance that governs our universe.

Arcane magic, the purest form of magical energy, stands in opposition to the unpredictable and often destructive Chaos magic. Basic magic, the foundation of all magical learning, finds its counter in the complex and time-bending Chronomancy which most regard as the most plot bending of all magics. Celestial magic, drawing its power from the cosmos and heavenly bodies, is opposed by the ground-bound and ancient Vozian magic. Dragon magic, a powerful and ancient form tied to the mythical creatures themselves, is in opposition to Nature magic, which draws its strength from the living world around us, and as many of us know dragons were the first of the outer planar beings to come to Shek. Elemental magic, harnessing the raw power of the natural elements, stands in contrast to the refined and structured Crystal magic. Life magic, the nurturing force that promotes growth and healing, finds its counter in the mechanical and precise Tech magic.

Understanding these oppositions is not merely an academic exercise. It is a journey towards understanding the fundamental laws that govern our universe and the magical energies that flow within it. As scholars of the arcane, we must strive to understand these relationships, not to exploit them, but to respect the delicate balance they represent and to harness our powers responsibly. Remember, dear students, in the grand scheme of the cosmos, opposition is not about conflict, but about balance and harmony.”

Path

Remember, as a mage, your journey is a balance of power and wisdom. Your potential is enormous, but the road to mastery is fraught with challenge. You're not merely casting spells - you're shaping reality. Choose your path wisely, and let your knowledge be your legacy.
