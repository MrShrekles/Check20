Weapons

Weapons and Customization

Weapons in Check20 are intentionally non-specific. They represent categories of combat tools rather than fixed items. This gives players creative freedom to describe and flavor their weapons while keeping the mechanics streamlined. Work with your Narrator to define what your weapon looks like, sounds like, and how it fits into your character’s story.

Once you’ve chosen a weapon, assign it to the proper category, and lock in its damage type based on what it is. A longsword would be a Standard Melee weapon with Slashing damage. A warhammer would be Impact, and a pick would deal Piercing. This applies across the board.

Additional Information

Check Damage: If a weapon lists Check for damage, deal damage equal to your total in the specified check instead of rolling dice.

If a weapon's damage includes an exclamation mark (e.g., 4d4!), it uses exploding dice. This means that for every die rolled at its maximum value, you roll an additional die of the same type and add it to the total. This can repeat indefinitely as long as max values keep being rolled.

Weapon Examples

  • Small Weapons: Daggers, hatchets, handaxes. Light, concealable, and quick to draw. Great for backups or stealthy strikes.
  • Standard Melee: Longswords, battleaxes, warhammers, war picks. One-handed martial weapons with solid, balanced design.
  • Standard Hefty: Greatswords, mauls, heavy flails. Massive two-handed weapons built for raw damage.
  • Reach Weapons: Polearms, spears, glaives, whips, chains. Melee weapons that extend your attack range.
  • Ranged Weapons: Bows, crossbows, revolvers, rifles, shotguns. Any weapon that fires a projectile, mundane or magical.
  • Thrown Weapons: Javelins, throwing axes, chakrams, heavy knives. Designed for short-range impact.
  • Exotic or Magical: Gut-powered sawblades, spectral revolvers, alchemical sprayers. These are stylized weapons that still follow category rules but may have unique properties or flair.
