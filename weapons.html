Weapons

Weapons and Customization

Weapons in Check20 are intentionally non-specific. They represent categories of combat tools rather than fixed items. This gives players creative freedom to describe and flavor their weapons while keeping the mechanics streamlined. Work with your Narrator to define what your weapon looks like, sounds like, and how it fits into your character’s story.

Once you’ve chosen a weapon, assign it to the proper category, and lock in its damage type based on what it is. A longsword would be a Standard Melee weapon with Slashing damage. A warhammer would be Impact, and a pick would deal Piercing. This applies across the board.

Weapon Examples

  • Small Weapons: Daggers, hatchets, handaxes. Light, concealable, and quick to draw. Great for backups or stealthy strikes.
  • Standard Melee: Longswords, battleaxes, warhammers, war picks. One-handed martial weapons with solid, balanced design.
  • Standard Hefty: Greatswords, mauls, heavy flails. Massive two-handed weapons built for raw damage.
  • Reach Weapons: Polearms, spears, glaives, whips, chains. Melee weapons that extend your attack range.
  • Ranged Weapons: Bows, crossbows, revolvers, rifles, shotguns. Any weapon that fires a projectile, mundane or magical.
  • Thrown Weapons: Javelins, throwing axes, chakrams, heavy knives. Designed for short-range impact.
  • Exotic or Magical: Gut-powered sawblades, spectral revolvers, alchemical sprayers. These are stylized weapons that still follow category rules but may have unique properties or flair.

Weapon Properties

  • Light: Lightweight and easy to wield, allowing for quick and fluid movements. Can be dual-wielded or used as an Off-Action.
  • Free Hands: Your hands are free and not occupied by the weapon.
  • Attached: This weapon is attached and cannot be disarmed.
  • Thrown: Can be used in melee or thrown for ranged attacks with short range.
  • Range: Weapons operate within specific ranges:
    • Melee: 5ft
    • Reach: 10ft
    • Short: Under 60ft
    • Medium: 60-220ft
    • Long: 220-600ft

    Optional rule: Attacking over half the range imposes disadvantage.

  • Hefty: Excessively heavy. Carrying more than two Hefty items causes the Pinned condition.
  • Reload: Requires reloading using an action, half-action, off-action, or movement.
  • Explosive: Causes powerful explosions that deal damage to all within range.
  • Hidden: Designed to be concealed, making detection difficult.
  • Mechanical: Can be disrupted, jammed, or broken.

Firearms

Firearms are rare and powerful tools in our world, often viewed with suspicion due to their potential for destruction. Most cities and populated areas regulate or even ban their use. Carrying a firearm openly attracts attention, especially from law enforcement or bandits. While firearms provide immense firepower, they come with risks and consequences. If you choose to wield one, do so wisely and carefully.

Special Firearms

  • Alchemist Launcher: Launch a potion at a target within short range using an Intellect check and a potion. On a success, it affects the target fully, and creatures within 5ft take half the effect.
  • Flamethrower: Check (Agi, Str, Obs); 2 Hand. Deals [[1d4!]] Fire damage to targets in a short-range line.

Firearm Properties

  • Loud: Firearms can be heard up to a mile away outdoors. They cause the Deaf condition to all creatures within short range. (Optional)
  • Reload: All firearms require reloading using an action, half-action, off-action, or movement. (Optional)
  • Mechanical: Firearms are mechanical devices that can jam or break.
  • Ammunition: All firearms require ammunition as described in the ammunition section.
  • Dual Wield: Dual-wielded firearms split damage dice between two targets.
