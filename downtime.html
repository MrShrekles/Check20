Merchant: Masters of Downtime

The Merchant class thrives not in battle, but in the spaces between. Downtime is their domain, where they build empires, craft unique goods, and influence the game world through cunning negotiation and resource management. Their impact on the narrative comes from creating opportunities, solving problems with commerce, and shaping the economy around them.

Merchant Conflict Hooks

As a merchant grows in power, their actions may disrupt local economies or provoke powerful factions. Here are potential challenges and hooks:

  • Criminal Syndicates: A faction sabotages operations with extortion or espionage, targeting trade routes or assets.
  • Merchant Guilds: Rival merchants or guilds lobby to impose regulations or dismantle monopolies.
  • Economic Collapse: Rapid expansion causes market instability, leading to price crashes or resource shortages.
  • Rebellion: Workers or customers revolt against unethical practices, requiring the merchant to address labor disputes or PR crises.
  • Political Intrigue: Nobles or governing bodies court the merchant for alliances, seeking influence over their wealth and network.

Merchant Reputation System

A merchant’s reputation shapes their interactions with the world, from loyal customers to envious rivals. Reputation can be tracked as a tiered system:

  • Tier 1 (Neutral): No special bonuses or penalties; standard market interactions.
  • Tier 2 (Respected): Merchants and buyers trust the player, offering minor discounts or favors.
  • Tier 3 (Trusted): Access to restricted markets, exclusive goods, or secret trade deals.
  • Tier 4 (Influential): Political figures or guilds seek alliances; rivals become increasingly hostile.
  • Tier 5 (Legendary): The merchant becomes a household name, influencing economies, nations, and even world events.

Merchant-Themed Quests

Merchants often drive quests that revolve around their goals or conflicts:

  • Supply Chain Disruption: Bandits or monsters block a critical trade route, halting business.
  • Hostile Takeover: A rival attempts to seize control of a merchant’s assets, requiring investigation or sabotage to counter.
  • Black Market Deals: The merchant must decide whether to engage in risky or illegal trade for immense profits.
  • Economic War: Compete against another merchant to dominate a market, leading to intrigue, sabotage, or alliances.
  • Crafting Challenge: A high-profile client commissions a unique item, requiring rare materials and tight deadlines.

Merchant Role in Party Dynamics

Merchants contribute to their party in diverse ways, emphasizing creativity and collaboration:

  • Resource Management: Merchants can provide access to rare items, healing potions, or magical gear.
  • Economic Leverage: Their wealth can bribe enemies, fund operations, or sway political outcomes.
  • Social Connections: Merchants often have extensive networks, offering the party access to information, favors, or safe havens.
  • Non-Combat Options: Merchants excel in resolving conflicts through negotiation, trade, or cunning schemes.

Merchant Downtime Activities

During downtime, merchants can engage in activities that enrich the narrative and offer unique gameplay opportunities:

  • Market Expansion: Establish new trade routes or expand operations into untapped regions. Success opens up new resources but may draw competition or attention from rival factions.
  • Crafting: Use skills like alchemy or blacksmithing to create unique items. High-quality or rare goods could attract buyers, leading to side quests or encounters.
  • Negotiation: Broker deals, forge alliances, or settle disputes. Successful negotiations might provide discounts, rare materials, or political favor.
  • Speculation: Invest in goods or businesses to manipulate the economy. Risk-reward scenarios can lead to significant profits—or devastating losses.
  • Infrastructure Projects: Build facilities like warehouses, workshops, or trade hubs. These structures provide tangible benefits, such as faster crafting or increased inventory capacity.
  • Merchant Intrigue: Hire spies or informants to gather intel on competitors, governments, or criminal organizations. This information could be leveraged for personal gain or to avoid conflict.
