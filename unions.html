Fabrication

A forge-born collective of smiths, machinists, crafters, and toolwrights.

Fabrication Unions build the bones of society—blades, bolts, hinges, and shields. Whether forging a sword, designing a firearm, or crafting mining tools, they value skill, consistency, and measurable results. Every item tells the maker’s story.

Opposition: Culture

Union Location: Foundries, machine shops, rail-line forges, or hardened craft halls heavy with smoke, hammering, and the scent of oiled leather.

Leaders (d4)

  • Belkin Varr: One-armed master smith, speaks only through apprentices
  • “Granite” Elga: Forewoman who drinks molten steel (or claims to)
  • Nello Skive: Precision-obsessed artificer with military contracts
  • Corrick Tom: Chatty tinker who’s secretly the best gunsmith in five cities

Union Names (d4)

  • The Ironright Union
  • Blackbrace Assembly
  • The Gritforge Chain
  • Hammermark Unionhall

Craft Goods

  • Reinforced Weapon: +1 to damage and can’t be disarmed for 1 day
  • Field Plate Patch: Temporary +10 armor and immunity to shove/push
  • Custom Grip: +1 to weapon checks made by the wielder
  • Gear Harness: Attach tools or weapons for fast switching in combat
  • Rivet Spike: Single-use trap or door wedge
  • Foldaway Shield: Compact, deployable, gives +5 armor once per combat

Merchant Features

  • Workbench Access: You may craft or modify a mundane weapon or tool once per rest
  • Blueprint Recall: Once per scene, declare that you “built that already” and produce a small, simple device
  • Union Tag: Your gear is marked with certified maker's stamps—sell for +25% in towns with a Fabrication presence
  • Scrap Reclaim: Break down 1 item per town to recover usable materials worth up to 25gp

Information Access

Members of the Fabrication Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Forge Order: Learn what items a local faction is ordering in bulk
  • Break Pattern: Identify a common flaw in a local enemy’s gear or defenses
  • Tool Route: Discover where material shipments or work crews are operating
Refinement

A league of brewers, alchemists, augmenters, and field engineers.

Refinement Unions solve problems through mixtures, tools, and tweaks. Whether concocting a stimulant, modifying a weapon, or reinforcing armor on the go, their methods are repeatable, mechanical, and strictly non-mystical.

Opposition: Mysticism

Union Location: Industrial labs, tonic dens, boiler sheds, or cluttered workshops—heavy with fumes, bubbling glass, and soot-slick aprons.

Leaders (d4)

  • Tanna Greaves: Alchemist with ink-stained sleeves and burned eyebrows
  • Eorl Wix: Gearcrafter who speaks in clicks and diagrams
  • Doc Bramble: Brewery engineer, always holding something bubbling
  • Sint Vatch: Modder-for-hire with a cough and too many tools

Union Names (d4)

  • The Copperstill Compact
  • Union of Applied Chemistry
  • The Augmenter’s Bench
  • Ironbrew Alliance

Refinement Stock

  • Reinforcing Draft: Temporarily grants +25 armor for 1 scene
  • Potent Stimulant: Target gains +1d4 to all checks for 10 minutes, then 1 Wound
  • Weapon Polish Kit: Adds +1 to a weapon’s damage rolls until rest
  • Alchemical Patch: Removes 1 Condition or stabilizes a Wounded ally
  • Smokecharge Pellet: Creates a 10ft smoke cloud, obscures vision
  • Stability Syringe: Once per session, ignore the next negative condition effect

Merchant Features

  • Portable Kit: Create an Upgrade Potion over a long rest that lasts for 1 week and can be applied to a weapon to increase its Check and Damage by +1 (once per week per weapon)
  • Field Tinkering: Once per day, modify a piece of gear for +3 to checks until a Long Rest
  • Formula Access: Brew any known alchemical item with one hour and proper materials
  • No-Frag Rule: Identify faulty or sabotaged items, traps, or brews on sight

Information Access

Members of the Refinement Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Tonic Trend: Learn which brews, drugs, or enhancements are being widely used or banned
  • Upgrade Request: Discover if any NPCs or factions are commissioning specific gear
  • Brew Scarcity: Learn which materials are in short supply for crafting in this region
Commerce

A cartel of traders, brokers, auctioneers, and financial liaisons.

Commerce Unions do not sell things, they sell access. They regulate pricing, control trade routes, and determine who is licensed to profit. Backroom deals, storefront licenses, and bulk contracts give them subtle dominance over every coin spent.

Opposition: Scholarship

Union Location: Trade halls, auction floors, Unionmarts, counting houses, or tiled offices packed with ledgers, trade permits, and tense conversations.

Leaders (d4)

  • Jentha Fale: Runs silent auctions with a crooked grin and a silver hammer
  • Mister Scrip: Never seen touching money, always knows who owes what
  • Mae Varn: Former merchant-ship captain, known for brutal efficiency
  • Burrim Quill: Middle-aged pen-pusher who somehow owns half the market

Union Names (d4)

  • The Unified Trade Ledger
  • Coinwright Consortium
  • The Merchant Axis
  • League of Open Markets

Trade Assets

  • Temporary Stall Permit: Grants the right to sell in any market for 24 hours
  • Margin Contract: Gain +25% profit on a single transaction
  • Buyout Clause: Remove a low-tier competitor from the market temporarily
  • Liquid Credit Slip: Spend this in place of 50gp, redeemable post-session
  • Escrow Bond: Secure a deal between two parties without full trust
  • Price Fix Ledger: Temporarily lock the value of one item at a chosen rate

Merchant Features

  • Market Leverage: Gain +1d4 to any Influence or Barter check involving trade
  • Preferred Vendor: You may buy one item per town at 25% off base value
  • License Holder: You may legally run a stall, shop, or brokerage in any town with a Commerce presence
  • Fast Exchange: Once per town, swap any item you own for one of equal value (narrator approved)

Information Access

Members of the Commerce Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Market Pulse: Learn what item or good is in demand (sells high)
  • Credit Risk: Discover whether a named NPC, shop, or faction is in debt
  • Trade Movement: See where large shipments are headed or delayed
Transport

A union of pilots, freight runners, railmasters, and route controllers.

Transport Unions control how people and goods move. They secure roads, maintain schedules, and determine who gets there first, or at all. Whether by zeppelin, mule, train, or boot, if it moves through civilized space, they likely signed off on it.

Opposition: Stewardship

Union Location: Freight yards, airship docks, cable car stations, rail depots, or dispatch towers, often with scattered crates, loudspeakers, and logbooks stacked to the ceiling.

Leaders (d4)

  • Lockjaw Dren: Ex-sky captain with a whistle always between her teeth
  • Cyrus Wain: Chain-smoking route clerk with perfect recall
  • Siggin Toll: Rail veteran who measures everything in time lost or gained
  • Eido Sash: Wears goggles even indoors, rarely speaks, always knows where to go

Union Names (d4)

  • The Stamped Manifest League
  • Steeltrail Authority
  • Union of Applied Motion
  • Clockturn Freight Chain

Transit Tools and Access

  • Transit Voucher: Free passage for your group on the next available route
  • Courier Tag: Grants priority delivery of a letter, item, or sealed crate
  • Detour Chart: Use this to avoid a known blockade, toll, or hazard
  • Shift Permit: Reroutes a standard transit route in your favor for one day
  • Emergency Recall Key: Summons a ride or transport to your location once
  • Hauler’s Ledger: Trade contacts or regional route info stored in shorthand

Merchant Features

  • Ticket Line: You can always secure basic travel for yourself and one ally, even in crisis
  • Cargo Claim: Once per region, reroute a crate or shipment to your location
  • Road Eyes: +1 to Navigation checks or travel planning
  • Waypost Access: You know the location of hidden, protected Union shelters along major routes

Information Access

Members of the Transport Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Route Forecast: Learn the fastest or safest path to a location
  • Disruption Log: See if any roads, tracks, or ports are under threat or delay
  • Manifest Glimpse: Learn who or what recently passed through a given transit point
Scholarship

An assembly of researchers, professors, archivists, and academic bureaucrats.

Scholarship Unions collect, preserve, and guard the written and spoken truths of the world. They maintain libraries, universities, observatories, and deep vaults of forbidden knowledge. Power is found in precision, precedent, and publishing first.

Opposition: Commerce

Union Location: Lecture halls, dusty archives, scriptoriums, printing houses, or quiet offices filled with filing cabinets, chalkboards, and stained mugs of tea.

Leaders (d4)

  • Headmaster Kilner: Elegant, ancient, and five steps ahead of any debate
  • Gresha Fen: Researcher who knows everything except when to stop talking
  • Dean of Ashes: Runs the restricted archives and never once smiled
  • Henrick Blot: Nervous editor with keys to a hundred sealed manuscripts

Union Names (d4)

  • Union of Indexed Studies
  • The Proofwork Circle
  • Lorekeeper Assembly
  • The Binding Shelf

Academic Resources

  • Verified Ledger: A sourcebook that grants +1 to research-related checks
  • Field Notes: Excerpted info that reveals the weakness or history of a threat
  • Contradiction File: Discredits a known claim or official narrative in the region
  • Draft Publication: Grants temporary prestige (+1 Influence in intellectual circles)
  • Referenced Map: Reveals a previously unknown location or access point
  • Scholar’s Favor: Trade this for help from an academic NPC, such as an assistant, translator, or contact

Merchant Features

  • Peer Review: Once per region, submit a paper and gain a +1 to Influence or Research rolls for the next 24 hours
  • Institutional Trust: You may access restricted records or archives in scholarly towns
  • Footnote Pull: You can ask one question per town and receive a vague but factual answer from local lorekeepers
  • Document Cache: Maintain a personal archive that stores clues, logs, or facts revealed through gameplay

Information Access

Members of the Scholarship Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Authoritative Source: Gain insight or confirmation on a specific piece of lore or mystery
  • Obscured Record: Uncover a suppressed, edited, or forgotten historical fact
  • Field Update: Learn what current research projects, expeditions, or academic disputes are active in the region
Mysticism

A formal network of artificers, wardwrights, relic brokers, and sanctioned casters.

Mysticism Unions regulate magic like electricity—powerful, dangerous, and taxable. They create wards, examine artifacts, and sell arcane access to those who can afford it. Equal parts esoteric service and magical licensing board, they do not tolerate freelance sorcery.

Opposition: Scholarship

Union Location: Marble towers, sigil-inscribed halls, warded vaults, underground sanctums, or public arcana counters with locked backrooms.

Leaders (d4)

  • Varn Lemein: Wears gloves over gloves, speaks only when properly addressed
  • Ariah of the Folded Eye: Knows too much, trusts nobody, reads auras like faces
  • Treve Magthorn: Sells relics with both charm and vague threats
  • High Ritualist Ink: Covered in glowing tattoos that map ley-lines

Union Names (d4)

  • The Ethermark Trust
  • Union of Regulated Thaumaturges
  • The Ledgered Eye
  • Warranted Circle of Binding

Arcane Goods

  • Mana Sigil: Charges a spell or item once, bypassing its normal cost or limit
  • Tether Stone: Anchors a person, object, or spirit to its current plane
  • Ward Kit: Temporary glyphs protect a room or entrance from magical intrusion
  • True Ink: Used to sign, seal, or alter magical contracts
  • Glimpse Orb: Shows a short magical memory from a creature or item
  • Curse Tag: Applies a minor affliction or hex to a target (must pass roll to resist)

Merchant Features

  • Arcane Tag: You may mark one item as sanctioned—it cannot be legally confiscated
  • Ley Access: You can tap into a known ley-line or mana source once per region
  • Sigil Trained: Gain +1 to checks involving magical tools or relics
  • Vault Key: Once per town, you may access Union storage to retrieve a tagged item, scroll, or relic worth up to 100 gp

Information Access

Members of the Mysticism Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Item Report: Learn the origin, last use, and danger level of a magical object
  • Spell Echo: Gain a ghost image of the last spell cast in an area
  • Warrant List: See who in the region is licensed, restricted, or forbidden from casting
Politics

A tangled web of lawmakers, administrators, clerks, and power brokers.

Politics Unions do not hold swords—they hold signatures, sealed files, and rubber stamps. Their strength lies in permits, legal delays, shifting ordinances, and the subtle rerouting of authority. They do not shout—they file.

Opposition: Culture

Union Location: Courthouses, permit offices, administrative towers, law libraries, or smoky backrooms where policy is bought more than it is made.

Leaders (d4)

  • Magistrate Kell: Always calm, never seen without gloves, reputation spotless
  • Darnet Quill: Bureaucrat turned puppeteer, “just a clerk” with 40 strings pulled
  • Silba Trask: Young reformer with dangerous charisma and old enemies
  • “The Whisperer”: Nobody’s seen them—just their directives, always stamped correctly

Union Names (d4)

  • The Charter Office
  • Graychain Committee
  • The Bureau of Unified Powers
  • The Policy Assembly

Bureaucratic Assets

  • Temporary Permit: Grants access to a restricted area or function for 24 hours
  • Sealed Order: Deliver this to reroute guards, pause an operation, or delay action
  • Arbitration Letter: Used to resolve (or suppress) disputes between Unions or factions
  • Official Seal: When shown, gives +1d4 to Influence or legal interaction rolls
  • Denial Notice: Blocks a rival’s action, project, or public gathering for a day
  • Amendment Copy: Allows rewriting or voiding a minor contract or law (with roll)

Merchant Features

  • Stamp Access: You may request one temporary permit per town for personal or party use
  • Soft Favor: Gain +1 Influence with bureaucrats, city guards, or legal factions
  • Delay Tactic: Once per day, stall an enemy or official action by one scene
  • Board Seat: You gain a named, if ceremonial, position—your votes shape local decisions

Information Access

Members of the Politics Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Active Motion: Learn what new law or Union policy is about to pass
  • Leverage File: Discover legal pressure points on a faction or leader
  • Permit Record: See who has recent permits for movement, meetings, or rallies
Culture

A network of performers, publishers, stylemakers, and behind-the-scenes handlers.

Culture Unions shape the public voice—through stage, page, broadcast, and spectacle. Whether they run opera houses, print tabloid gossip, or choreograph street protests, their work moves people with emotion, color, and spin.

Opposition: Politics

Union Location: Theaters, nightclubs, printshops, cabarets, and radio stations; usually surrounded by lights, noise, and cigarette haze.

Leaders (d4)

  • Felix Bloom: Veteran playwright turned kingmaker with a velvet scarf
  • Dame Letta: Opera singer, now producer, who talks like she’s still on stage
  • Mr. Paddock: Silent film director known for explosive scandals
  • Minnie Crim: Gossip queen and editor of the Sablepier Echo

Union Names (d4)

  • The Veil & Varnish Society
  • Union of the Open Stage
  • Radiowave 12
  • The Paperroom

Cultural Tools

  • Flash Poster Run: Quickly spreads a message, slogan, or face through a district
  • Performance Permit: Grants the right to speak, sing, or protest in a public space
  • Blacklist Ledger: Contains the names of those recently canceled or slandered
  • Radio Segment: Brief broadcast used to influence morale or perception in a town
  • Agent Contract: Grants temporary access to a loyal stagehand, hype agent, or journalist
  • Costuming Kit: Grants +1d4 to a disguise, impersonation, or on-stage deception

Merchant Features

  • Spotlight: Gain +1 Favor in any scene where you perform or make a public display
  • Whispersheet: Once per day, seed a rumor or story into local news—true or false
  • Access Pass: Always allowed into entertainment venues, lounges, and social hubs
  • Stagehand: You gain a personal assistant NPC who specializes in distraction or flare

Information Access

Members of the Culture Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Trending Topic: Learn what’s capturing local attention or stirring unrest
  • Public Sentiment: Gain insight into a location’s attitude toward a group or event
  • Media Leverage: Learn if any public figures are vulnerable to exposure or influence
Martial

A disciplined network of professional enforcers, bounty hunters, and hired blades.

Martial Unions keep cities clean, protect shipments, and ensure contracts are honored, sometimes with bullets, sometimes with bodies. They operate on rigid structure, ironclad deals, and a public image of strength. Their presence is usually loud, uniformed, and undeniable.

Opposition: Subversion

Union Location: Fortified compounds, contract boards, city guard annexes, or private security towers staffed by trench-coated veterans, steel-eyed clerks, and the occasional retired war mage.

Leaders (d4)

  • Captain Derrow: Straight-backed old vet, loyal to a fault
  • "Mad" Brisa Tull: Whiskey-drinking bounty hunter turned organizer
  • The Sergeant: Anonymous, face always covered, speaks through others
  • Benny Hooks: Ex-mafia enforcer legitimized by a badge

Union Names (d4)

  • Ironwright Contract Agency
  • The 3rd Line Brigade
  • Crowhall Security
  • Brassplate Syndicate

NPCs for Hire

The Martial Unions will often have a set of NPCs available for hire. For example:

  • Soldier (PL10): Sword, sidearm, heavy armor
  • Spellsword (PL8): Sword, basic origin, light armor
  • Brute (PL7): Greatsword, +100 armor, shock gloves
  • Two Crows (PL6): Twin saboteurs, knives, smoke bombs
  • Dogpack (PL6): Three trained hounds; tracking and melee
  • Rifleman (PL5): Long rifle, trick shots, smoke bombs

Merchant Section

  • Hireling: Gain a permanent PL5 mercenary
  • Gearwork: +1 to weapon and armor checks and damage
  • Command Chain: Once per day, issue a verbal command; one ally gains +1d4 to a combat roll
  • Shield Contract: Your group is flagged as under protection; guards give +1 Favor in towns with Martial presence

Information

Members of the Martial Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Active Bounty: Get the name, last known location, and reward for a regional target
  • Union Watchlist: Learn if any local NPCs or factions are flagged as hostile, criminal, or under surveillance
  • Safe Route: Get a guarded or conflict-free travel path to the next settlement or objective
Subversion

Subversion Unions thrive in shadows, turning secrets into power and crime into currency. Their halls are hidden speakeasies, private basements, or front businesses, places where nothing is off-limits and everything has a price.

Opposition: Martial

Union Location: Backroom parlors, boarded-up offices, smoke-filled bars, and underground lounges thick with hush money and whispered deals.

Harvest

A collective of farmers, miners, foragers, and resource gatherers.

Harvest Unions are the lifeblood of regional supply, trading sweat for sustenance. They manage fields, forests, quarries, and fisheries—bringing raw goods to market and controlling who gets to eat, build, or burn. Where the Martial Unions guard the cities, the Harvest Unions keep the wilds habitable.

Opposition: Commerce

Union Location: Sawmills, smokehouses, farm co-ops, quarry lodges, or smoke-stained bunkhouses lined with old tools and fresh dirt.

Leaders (d4)

  • Cobb Verren: One-eyed cattle boss with a boot for every season
  • Gretta Stonehook: Former miner, face lined with soot and smile-wrinkles
  • Marl Patch: Nomadic herbalist who speaks in local dialects
  • Ramel Brine: Fisherman who never steps far from the riverbank

Union Names (d4)

  • Rootwell Cooperative
  • The Ironfork Assembly
  • Grainspike Union
  • Ash & Ore Federation

Harvest Assets

  • Crate of Raw Goods: 1 unit of tradeable material (grain, ore, lumber, etc.)
  • Forager’s Bundle: Rare herbs, pelts, or bone—sells high or aids crafting
  • Extraction Permit: Legal access to restricted land for gathering
  • Pack Beasts: Temporary increase to travel carry capacity or speed
  • Field Tools: Reduces fatigue for physical labor or speeds up harvesting
  • Smuggled Harvest: Illegal goods pulled from contested or cursed land

Merchant Features

  • Supply Runner: You can source 1 unit of basic material per town
  • Beast Bond: You gain a work animal (mule, ox, giant beetle) that can carry, defend, or assist
  • Field Broker: Sell gathered resources at +25% value in towns with a Harvest presence
  • Claim Flag: Mark a wild zone as claimed by your Union—local poachers or rivals may face penalties or resistance

Information Access

Members of the Harvest Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Resource Spot: Discover a new viable harvest site within your current region
  • Territory Dispute: Learn if another Union is interfering or illegally extracting nearby
  • Weather Sign: Gain insight into incoming natural shifts (blight, drought, migration, storm)
Stewardship

A coalition of foremen, civil engineers, sanitation directors, and land stewards.

Stewardship Unions ensure the cities don’t fall apart at the seams. From managing irrigation canals and sewer lines to rationing coal and rerouting food trucks, they handle the unseen rhythms of civilization. Quietly vital, and they are quick to remind you of it.

Opposition: Subversion (destabilizers)

Union Location: Utility stations, old city maintenance yards, transit hubs, grain depots, or repurposed town halls filled with clipboards and coal smoke.

Leaders (d4)

  • Gilda Cask: Hard-nosed forewoman who carries a steam wrench like a badge
  • Rameel Moss: Environmental planner who speaks in long-winded metaphors
  • Red Eir: Local legend who remembers every pipeline and street by heart
  • Inspector Knox: Pencil-mustached bureaucrat, perfectly clean gloves

Union Names (d4)

  • The Civic Flow Bureau
  • Steamline Authority
  • Grain & Iron Compact
  • Wardens of the Ledger

Infrastructure Assets

  • Emergency Ration Vouchers: Gain access to food, water, or energy for your group
  • Map of City Lines: Learn a shortcut, tunnel, or bypass route (ignores obstacles once)
  • Work Crew Favor: Get temporary laborers for repairs, barricades, or hauling
  • Inspection Permit: Grants legal access to restricted zones for 1 day
  • Portable Generator: Lights a scene, powers a device, or delays collapse
  • Water Purity Kit: Neutralizes a poison, toxin, or environmental hazard in a small area

Merchant Features

  • Civic Trust: +1 Influence with towns, utilities, and rural leaders
  • Repair Access: You can reroute, stabilize, or bypass infrastructure once per session
  • Supply Chain Tag: You may request basic goods (food, materials) at no cost once per town
  • Outpost License: You may designate one player-owned building as an official service point (grants +1 Favor with locals)

Information Access

Members of the Stewardship Union can request up to one report per visit:

  • Resource Strain: Learn which settlements or neighborhoods are running low on key supplies
  • Infrastructure Risk: Get early warnings on upcoming collapses, floods, or shortages
  • Zone Report: Determine which factions have logistical control over a target district
