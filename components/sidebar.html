    Roll History

      Action Types
      • Move: Up to your Speed
      • Action: The primary activity you perform, like attacking or casting a spell. You get one action per turn, though certain abilities may offer more.
      • Half-Action: Instead of a single action, take 2 half-actions. These are things like picking things up or Stances
      • Off-Action: Can be used on your turn or after. Most used for Provoke attacks or light weapon strikes
      • Non-Action: Talk, switch items, other
      • Finisher: Eliminate locked or submitted foes
      Actions
      • Attack: Make a melee or ranged attack. Unarmed deal 1 dmg
      • Grab or Hold Contested Physical Check (Strength Default)
      • Cast Spell: Requires seeing and perceiving the target, and having free hands.
      • Administer Potion: Use an action to give a potion to a downed ally.
      • Stealth: Attempt to hide for advantage on the next attack.
      Half-Actions
      • Stances: Choose a stance to remain in until your next turn.
      • Disarm: Make a contested Strength or Agility check to disarm a creature.
      • Help: Give a creature +1 to a check
      • Pick up an Item: Picking up an item is a Half-Action.
      • Use an Object: Interact with objects like levers or buttons.
      • Disengage: Move out of a creature's range without provoking opportunity attacks.
      Off-Actions
      • Provoke: Make an attack against a creature that has missed an attack directly targeting you, or against a creature that willingly leaves your melee weapon range.
      • Block: As an Off-Action, reduce incoming damage by half if you have Armor from a shield
      • Demoralize: Make a contested Influence check against an intelligent creature. On a success, the target gains the Fear condition.
      Non-Actions
      • Switch Weapons: Drop a weapon and draw another.
      • Stress Push: Take 4 stress damage for +3 on a low roll.
      • Roleplay: Briefly communicate or give orders.
      Stances
      • Advantage Stance: Gain advantage on your next attack.
      • Disadvantage Stance: Give disadvantage to enemies attacking you or allies in range.
      • Guard Stance: When a creature attacks you, Provoke without using an Off-Action once. On all failed attacks, you may also Provoke at disadvantage.
      Special
      • Called Shot: −10 for a condition.
      • Surprise: Additional action.
      • Cover: Disadvantage to be hit.
      • Full Cover: Cannot be targeted without line of sight.
      Movement
      • Difficult Terrain: Halved speed.
      • Jump: 2ft × Agility or Strength.
      • Climb/Swim: Counts as difficult terrain.
      • Prone: Disadv on ranged attacks against you; adv on melee. Standing costs 15ft.
      • Moving Past: Can only pass smaller creatures.
      • Dash: Double distance (Action).
      • Fall: 5 dmg per 10ft.
      • Extreme Hazard: 10 dmg/sec.
      • Burrowing: Unseen. Cannot target or breathe.
      • Teleport: Unoccupied space; no opportunity attacks.
      Size Scale
      • Tiny: < 1 ft; pixies, potions
      • Small: 1–4 ft; goblins
      • Medium: 5–7 ft; humans
      • Large: 8–15 ft; ogres
      • Giant: 15–25 ft; siege equipment
      • Monolithic: 25–100 ft; building
      Press On

      After a combat encounter, characters may Press On to recover:

      • 1 class resource and 1 spell point
      • Armor repair: [[1d6]] + Crafting
      • Healing: [[1d6]] + Survival
      • Remove all conditions and effects lasting 1 minute or less

      Press On may be used twice per day.

      Long Rest

      Requires 10 hours of uninterrupted downtime in a safe environment. Restores:

      • All wounds
      • All class resources and spell points
      • Armor repaired to at least half
      • Remove all conditions lasting one day or less

      Can only be taken once per day.

      • 25–29: Success++ (2 rolls)
      • 30–34: Success+++ (3 rolls)

      Corpus Conditions
      Bleeding Cannot recover wounds or receive healing. Lasts 1 minute
      Broken Physical Disadvantage Lasts 6 weeks
      Concussion Mental Disadvantage, Spellcasting Disadvantage Until a Long Rest
      Coughing Mental Disadvantage Until a Press On or irritant removed
      Dislocation Physical Disadvantage Until a Long Rest, or Survival Check
      Slowed Movement halved Until source removed
      Pinned Cannot move; restrained by an object or creature. Until source removed, or Off-Action removal
      Prone Disadvantage on ranged attacks; advantage for melee attacks against you. Requires 15 ft of movement to stand.
      Cognition Conditions
      Blind Your attacks have -5, attacks against you have +5; You cannot target creatures with spells Off-Action removal if possible or permanent effects require a healer
      Charmed Mental Disadvantage; cannot attack the source of the charm. Ends based on the charm's source
      Confused Mental Disadvantage, attacks against you have advantage. Until end of next turn or Press On
      Deaf Stealth disadvantage; Spellcasting disadvantage Lasts 1 minute or source removed
      Fear Must dash away or hide until the end of your next turn. Ends if you cannot see the source for a full round; typically cannot be the same source twice.
      Special Conditions
      Intangible Immune to physical damage; cannot attack non Intangible; movement halved. Ends after the effect's duration or through magical disruption. Cannot move through inorganic material.
      Invisible Cannot be targeted by Provoke; attacks against have disadvantage. Ends after making an aggressive action or when revealed by substances like powder or oil.
      Major Conditions
      Stunned Disadvantage on all checks; movement halved. Until end of next turn or Press On
      Exhaustion Disadvantage on all checks; movement halved. Persists until a long rest, magical recovery, or specific intervention (e.g., caffeine).
      Constrained Cannot make attack actions; attacks against have advantage Off-Action removal if possible, escape check from restraints
      Unconscious Unable to act; vulnerable to critical hits and Finishers. Ends after a set duration, damage taken, or Off-Action ally assistance.
      Exposed Take double damage Until end of next turn or Press On
      Injured At 0 wounds, any further damage causes immediate Death. Healing removes the condition. Excess damage beyond reducing wounds to 0 is ignored and does not carry over and cause death
      Death Character dies and cannot interact with the living world. Only resurrection magic can remove this condition.

      Realm Rules

      🌍 Realm Rules (Work in Progress)

      These realm-specific effects shape rest, travel, magic, and resistance. This list is a living draft—expect updates as planar rules expand.

      • Prime Realm 🌐
        The natural world. Standard mechanics apply. Rests, Realm Shifting, and spellcasting function normally.
      • Fey Realm ✨
        Long Rests require 32 hours. Charm effects cannot be resisted or saved against.
      • Eclipse 🌑
        You may Realm Shift into the Eclipse, but may only exit into Ordealis.
      • Ordealis 🔥
        Realm Shift is only possible from the Eclipse. You cannot leave unless granted passage by a Rotalisk or Asmodeus.
      • Dreamsea 🌊
        (Rules in development.) Expected to involve sleep, memory, illusions, and emotion-driven effects.
      • Void 🌀
        Astral travel to and from this realm is easy. Teleportation spells are more effective and stable.
      • Abyss 🩸 (includes Falling Grounds & God Prison)
        You cannot Realm Shift into the Abyss by normal means.
        • Falling Grounds 🕊️ – Holy magic costs double mana; celestial beings act erratically.
        • God Prison 🔒 – Divine intervention and god-granted spells automatically fail.
        • Abyssal Depths – Time fractures, healing spells may corrupt, and Wound accumulation is increased.
