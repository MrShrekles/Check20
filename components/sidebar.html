    Roll History

      What to do on your turn
      • Move: Move any distance up to your speed during your turn.
      • Action: Perform main abilities like attack or cast a spell.
      • Half-Action: Perform two minor actions instead of 1 action, like switching stances.
      • Off-Action (Reaction): Additional attacks with light weapons or reaction to a missed attack.
      • Non-Action: Quick tasks like talking that require minimal effort.
      • Finisher: Eliminate defenseless opponents locked from combat.

        • Positioning in Combat

      • Cover: Disadvantage to hit. Full cover cannot be targeted.
      • Range: Melee, reach (10ft), short (under 50ft), medium (50–200ft), long (200+ft).
      Types of Movement
      • Regular: Move up to your speed.
      • Dash: Move double distance as an action.
      • Jumping: 5ft per check (2× with running start).
      • Climbing: Requires agility checks; risk of falling.
      • Difficult Terrain / Swimming: Halves movement speed.
      • Prone: Standing up uses 15ft movement.
      • Moving Past Creatures: Only allies or small enemies.
      • Falling: 5 impact damage per 10ft.
      • Extreme Conditions: Hazardous environments deal 10 damage per second.
      • Burrowing: Full cover while burrowed, but no breathing.
      Actions
      • Attack: Make a combat attack.
      • Unarmed Strike: Deal minor damage without weapons.
      • Grab or Hold: Grapple, contested Strength or Agility.
      • Stealth: Hide or conceal presence.
      • Cast a Spell: Perform magical actions.
      • Dash: Move double distance as an action.
      • Administer a Potion: Heal or aid an ally.
      Half-Actions & Stances

        Stances:

      • Advantage Stance: Give your next attack advantage.
      • Disadvantage Stance: Enemies have disadvantage to hit you and nearby allies.
      • Offensive Stance: React with an opportunity attack when attacked.
      • Reaction Stance: React to any failed attacks against you.

        • Half Actions:

      • Pick up an Item: Retrieve something in combat.
      • Use an Object: Interact with your environment.
      • Escape: Break free from restraints or conditions.
      • Disarm: Remove an opponent's weapon.
      • Help: Grant advantage to one ally on a check.
      • Disengage: Avoid opportunity attacks.
      • Shove: Knock a creature prone via contest.
      • Taunt: Force a check; failure compels enemy to target you.
      Off Actions
      • Reaction: In response to another action.
      • Opportunity Attacks: Strike when enemies drop their guard.
      • Drink Something: Quickly consume something for benefit.
      • Disrupt: Give disadvantage on a successful check.
      • Block: If shielded, halve melee damage with a reaction.
      • Demoralize: Intimidate to force morale check or flee.
      Non-Actions
      • Limited to 1 per turn. Minimal effort actions.
      • Stress Push: Take 4 stress to boost a failed check up to 15.
      • Switch Weapons: Swap gear in combat.
      • Roleplay: Speak or act in character briefly.
      • Shouting Orders: Command allies or intimidate foes.
      Special Actions
      • Called Shots: Take -10 to apply a condition.
      • Surprise Turns: Gain extra action in specific conditions.
      • Targeting: Use senses to engage enemies (part of an action).
      • Cover: Use cover for protection (part of movement).
      Size
      • Tiny: Under 1 ft (insects, potion bottles).
      • Small: 1–4 ft (cats, scrolls).
      • Medium: 5–7 ft (humanoids, armor).
      • Large: 8–15 ft (bears, shields).
      • Giant: 15–25 ft (elephants, siege engines).
      • Monolithic: 25–100 ft (castles, airships).
      • Environmental: 100–300 ft (mountains, landmarks).

      Conditions

      Condition Effect Duration / Description
      Corpus Conditions
      Bleeding Cannot recover wounds or receive healing. Lasts 1 minute
      Broken Physical Disadvantage Lasts 6 weeks
      Concussion Mental Disadvantage, Spellcasting Disadvantage Until a Long Rest
      Coughing Mental Disadvantage Until a Press On or irritant removed
      Dislocation Physical Disadvantage Until a Long Rest, or Survival Check
      Slowed Movement halved Until source removed
      Pinned Cannot move; restrained by an object or creature. Until source removed, or Off-Action removal
      Prone Disadvantage on ranged attacks; advantage for melee attacks against you. Requires 15 ft of movement to stand.
      Cognition Conditions
      Blind Disadvantage on attack checks; attacks against you have advantage. Off-Action removal if possible or permanent effects require a healer
      Charmed Mental Disadvantage; cannot attack the source of the charm. Ends based on the charm's source
      Confused Mental Disadvantage, attacks against you have advantage. Until end of next turn or Press On
      Deaf Stealth disadvantage; Spellcasting disadvantage Lasts 1 minute or source removed
      Fear Must dash away or hide until the end of your next turn. Ends if you cannot see the source for a full round; typically cannot be the same source twice.
      Special Conditions
      Intangible Immune to physical damage; cannot attack; movement halved. Ends after the effect's duration or through magical disruption. Cannot move through inorganic material.
      Invisible Cannot be targeted by opportunity attacks; attacks against have disadvantage. Ends after making an aggressive action or when revealed by substances like powder or oil.
      Unconscious Unable to act; vulnerable to critical hits and Finishers. Ends after a set duration, damage taken, or Off-Action ally assistance.
      Major Conditions
      Stunned Disadvantage on all checks; movement halved. Until end of next turn or Press On
      Exhaustion Disadvantage on all checks; movement halved. Persists until a long rest, magical recovery, or specific intervention (e.g., caffeine).
      Constrained Cannot make attack actions; attacks against have advantage Off-Action removal if possible, escape check from restraints
      Exposed Take double damage Until end of next turn or Press On
      Injured At 0 wounds, any further damage causes immediate Death. Healing removes the condition. Excess damage beyond reducing wounds to 0 is ignored and does not carry over and cause death
      Death Character dies and cannot interact with the living world. Only resurrection magic can remove this condition.
